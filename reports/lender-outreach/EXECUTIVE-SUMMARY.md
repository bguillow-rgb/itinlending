# ITIN Lender Partner Search — Executive Summary
**Prepared for ITINLending.net · 2026-07-04**

## The one thing to know
Your offer (one exclusive lending partner, 60-day pilot) fits **direct retail lenders** who already buy their own leads. It does **not** fit lead marketplaces, which resell non-exclusively. So the target list splits into three lanes, and you should work them differently:

1. **Exclusive-pilot anchors (direct retail lenders)** — your real prize. Pitch the exclusive 60-day pilot.
2. **Lead marketplaces (LeadsMarket, LeadNetwork, Engine, LendingTree)** — fastest money, but non-exclusive. Use these to monetize overflow, not as your anchor.
3. **Card issuers (Capital One, Chase)** — buy only through CPA/affiliate networks (CJ/Impact). Route ITIN card applicants per-approval; no bespoke exclusive deal.

## Fast-cash alternative (if pilot exclusivity is negotiable)
**LeadsMarket** and **LeadNetwork** are pure pay-per-lead marketplaces that will onboard you as a publisher and pay weekly (LeadsMarket cites up to 90% rev share). They buy personal/auto/business loan leads and are the quickest path to revenue — but they resell, so they can't be your "exclusive" anchor. Best use: sell them the flow you can't place exclusively.

## Hard truths from the research
- **Verified personal emails barely exist.** Across ~30 companies, exactly **one** decision-maker email was literally published: `lspenle@liftfund.com` (LiftFund). Everything else is a role inbox, a partnership form, a phone, or LinkedIn. I did not guess a single address. This is normal for BD at this level, and it matches your "verified over questionable" instruction.
- **The CDFI tier is mostly a mismatch.** Accion, LiftFund, Justine Petersen, Ascendus, DreamSpring lend to *small businesses*, not consumers. They're referral-partnership prospects for ITIN *entrepreneur* leads, not buyers of consumer leads. Capital Good Fund and Mission Asset Fund are the exceptions (real ITIN consumer lending) but are mission-driven and unlikely to *pay*.
- **Several ITIN claims still need a primary-source check** before you pitch: OppFi, Westlake, Exeter, Santander (auto ITIN is a dealer-level decision, not published), Newfi and Change (mortgage). Flagged per-row in the sheet.
- **Three "ITIN card" names are dead:** Petal (folded into Empower/Tilt), Deserve (consumer card closing, sold to Intuit), Tomo (card discontinued). Don't waste time there.
- **Auto lenders buy through dealers, not corporate lead desks** — except Santander's RoadLoans, which runs a direct-to-consumer funnel and is the one auto name worth a direct pilot conversation.
