# ITIN Consumer Lending — Investment-Grade Business Plan & Legal Roadmap

**Prepared:** 2026-07-05 · **For:** Owner/operator of ITINLending.net (New York) · **Purpose:** fastest *compliant* path from 27 in-hand ITIN consumer-loan applications to a real specialty-finance operation.

> **This is a business/operator analysis, not legal advice.** Consumer lending is among the most heavily regulated U.S. activities. Every legal conclusion below is cited so you can verify it, but you must engage (a) licensed **consumer-finance counsel** and (b) **state licensing counsel** before originating a single loan. Points the research flagged as uncertain or fast-moving (CFPB funding, DIDMCA/true-lender litigation, CTA/BOI final rule, TCPA consent) are marked **[VERIFY]**.

## 0. Executive Summary

**The blunt finding:** You cannot compliantly fund your 27 applications directly and quickly, for two structural reasons: (1) consumer-lending licensure follows the **borrower's state of residence** ([CSBS/NMLS](https://www.csbs.org/nationwide-multistate-licensing-system-nmls)), and your 27 applicants are spread across many states, each its own license; and (2) you are in **New York**, whose **16% civil / 25% criminal usury caps** (NY Gen. Oblig. Law §5-501; NY Penal Law §190.40) make a profitable sub-$5k subprime loan to a New Yorker *illegal to originate*. New York is the single worst U.S. state for this product, and NY DFS is the most aggressive regulator in the country ([American Banker, 2026](https://www.americanbanker.com/news/as-cfpb-retreats-state-ags-and-bank-regulators-step-up)).

**The recommended path is capital-first, license-second, three lanes running in parallel:**

1. **Refer the 27 now** to the licensed ITIN lenders/loan officers you already have drafted. Zero licensing, revenue this week, keeps applicants warm.
2. **Deploy your capital as a lender *to* a licensed lender** (warehouse line / loan participation) — a **business-purpose loan to a company is not consumer lending**, so no consumer license and NY's consumer usury caps don't apply. This is the *fastest legal way to put money to work* and is attainable with no track record.
3. **Build the company** by licensing in **one permissive anchor state** (Utah's notification-only Consumer Credit Notification, or Missouri's Consumer Installment Lender license), originating **only to that state's residents**, priced **≤36% APR** to a **near-prime-underwritten** ITIN borrower. Operate from NY; never lend to New Yorkers.

**Why ≤36% and near-prime:** at a compliant 36% APR, sub-$5k *subprime* loans barely break even after 15–25% charge-offs — that is exactly why OppFi/Enova charge 130–200% APR ([OppFi ~136% avg APR, ~43% annual net charge-offs](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001818502/000181850225000022/a2025q2earningspresentat.htm)) and why Oportun stays near 36% only by underwriting to **near-prime** and absorbing ~12% losses ([Oportun FY2024](https://investor.oportun.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/148/oportun-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2024-financial)). Your durable edge is **underwriting quality**, not rate.

**Corporate structure:** Keep ITINLending.net (media/lead-gen) in your existing LLC. Form a **separate single-purpose lending subsidiary** ("NewCo Lending LLC") so the licensed lender is ring-fenced from the marketing entity. As a US-formed LLC, NewCo currently owes **no CTA/BOI filing** ([FinCEN IFR, 90 Fed. Reg. 13688, Mar. 26, 2025](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/03/26/2025-05199/beneficial-ownership-information-reporting-requirement-revision-and-deadline-extension)) **[VERIFY no final rule has superseded]**.

## 0.5 Lead-Data Reality Check & Volume Viability (added 2026-07-05, from the real lead sheet)

The plan above was drafted against the brief's premise ("27 applications, avg $4,000, mostly under $5,000"). **The actual lead export breaks that premise.** Of 24 rows spanning ~one month (2026-06-06 to 2026-07-04):

| Segment | Count | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Genuine loan inquiries** (personal/business) | **~11** | Not 27 |
| Credit-**card** seekers | 8 | Different product; cannot be "funded" as loans — route to card issuers (Capital One/Chase CPA) |
| Credit-**score** checks | 2 | Not fundable |
| Test / junk / duplicate | ~3–4 | "gdgdtdh", "fff", "ccccc", "John Doe" ($25k, flagged test) |

**Three brief assumptions the data breaks:**
1. **"Mostly under $5,000" is false.** Of ~11 loan leads, only 4 are under $5k; 4 are **$5–10k**; 1 is **$25k+**. Average is well above $4,000.
2. **Scattered across ~8 states, none in an anchor state** (CO, GA×2, WY, TX×2, FL, MA, IL, NC, TN — zero in UT/MO). Confirms: licensing one anchor state does **not** help fund *these* leads. Colorado lead is sub-580 in a hard 36% cap → uneconomic.
3. **Two cleanest leads are business loans** (NC $25k+, TN $5–10k) — the *easiest* to fund directly (business-purpose credit is largely exempt from consumer licensing/usury).

**Routing for this exact list:** personal loans → refer to licensed lenders/LOs; the 2 business loans → fund directly (with counsel) or refer to a business lender; 8 card leads → Capital One/Chase CPA; score checks → nurture; Colorado sub-580 → decline/refer. **Both NY leads are credit cards, not loans** — so NY usury doesn't even bite here.

### Can ~27 leads/month (flat, no growth) be a business?

**As a direct LENDER: no — not at this volume, and not close.** A compliant lender carries fixed monthly overhead even when tiny — LMS, KYC/data vendors, ACH, servicing labor, and amortized counsel/compliance — realistically **~$3,000–$5,000/month** to run lean. Net contribution per funded near-prime loan is only **~$25–35/month** (from the Phase 7 economics). Covering ~$4k/month of overhead therefore needs **~120–160 active loans**, i.e. **~10–13 new funded loans every month, steady-state**. Working backwards through a thin ITIN funnel (only leads in your licensed state count; ~30% approve after tighter underwriting), that requires on the order of **~100–150 *loan* leads/month concentrated in your licensed states** — roughly **10–15× the current ~11/month, and geographically concentrated**, which today they are not. **At ~1–2 fundable loans/month, fixed costs dwarf interest income. A lending business is not viable at flat current volume.**

**As a LEAD-GEN / referral business: yes, but it's a modest side income today, not a company — and it scales linearly with your SEO.** ~19 monetizable inquiries/month (~11 loan + ~8 card). At industry-typical exclusive-lead economics **[estimate — validate with real buyers; your outreach drafts are the price-discovery mechanism]** — say ~$40–80 per exclusive loan lead and per-approval CPA on cards — current monetization is on the order of **~$500–$1,500/month**. That covers tools and hosting; it is not yet a business. It becomes a **real** lead-gen business (~$10k+/month) at roughly **10–20× volume**, which is exactly what ranking gains across all three sites can deliver over 12–24 months — and unlike lending, **every incremental lead is worth money from dollar one, with no licensing, no capital at risk, and no charge-offs.**

**The staged conclusion (reinforces the whole plan):**
1. **Today (current volume):** the viable model is **lead-gen/referral** — sell/refer the loan leads, CPA the card leads. Asset-light, compliant, scales with SEO.
2. **As loan volume grows and concentrates (~100+ loan leads/mo):** become the **capital behind a licensed lender** (Lane 2 participation) to capture lending margin without the full origination build.
3. **At real scale + single-state concentration:** stand up **direct origination** (Lane 4).

**Do not build a direct-lending company off 27 leads/month.** Build the **lead-gen engine** now (it's already working and it compounds), monetize immediately by referral/CPA, and let *concentrated* volume — not hope — trigger the move into lending.

### 2C. State law (the binding constraint)

- **License where the borrower lives.** ([CSBS 50-state survey](https://www.csbs.org/50-state-survey-consumer-finance-laws); NY, NE, OR statutes are borrower-resident-scoped.) One state = one regulator, one bond, one rate table.

- **Anchor-state candidates (fast + workable rate):**

- **Avoid at launch:** CO/SD/NE/MT (36% ballot caps kill small-dollar economics — [NCLC 2025](https://www.nclc.org/resources/predatory-installment-lending-in-the-states-2025/)); California (heavier CFL regime); New York.

## 3. PHASE 3 — Underwriting Manual & Scorecard (built from scratch)

**Design principle:** ITIN borrowers are often **thin-file / no-SSN-hit**, so **bank-transaction cash-flow underwriting is your primary signal**, supplemented by alt-data bureaus and (for immigrants) cross-border credit. Underwrite to **near-prime** to keep losses ~10–13% and stay profitable at ≤36%.

### Data sources
- **Cash-flow (primary):** Plaid or Finicity/Mastercard — 3–6 months of deposit/expense history substitutes for a score ([Plaid cash-flow underwriting](https://plaid.com/blog/cash-flow-underwriting-industry-standard/)).
- **Alt-data bureau:** **Clarity Services (Experian)** — subprime/thin-file specialty bureau ([Experian AFS](https://www.experian.com/alternative-financial-services/)).
- **Immigrant credit:** **Nova Credit** Credit Passport (translates foreign credit history) ([Nova Credit](https://www.novacredit.com/credit-passport)).
- Core SSN-keyed bureau pulls will mostly no-hit ITIN borrowers — don't rely on them.

### Verification stack
- **Identity (Red Flags + fraud):** Persona (explicit **ITIN/TIN verification** against IRS) or Socure (hard-to-identify populations) + **SentiLink** for synthetic-ID; **device fingerprinting** via the fraud vendor.
- **ITIN verification:** validate the ITIN letter + unexpired gov ID + proof of address (documentary), plus vendor TIN check.
- **Bank account:** Plaid account + balance + **NACHA WEB-debit account validation** before first ACH.
- **OFAC:** screen name/DOB against SDN list at origination.

### Approval matrix (illustrative — calibrate to your loss target)

| Factor | Minimum / rule |
|---|---|
| **Minimum income** | $1,800/mo verified via bank deposits |
| **Employment/income length** | ≥3 months consistent deposit history |
| **DTI (incl. new loan payment)** | ≤45%; ≤40% for larger tiers |
| **Housing payment** | Verified rent/mortgage present & current in bank data |
| **Cash-flow** | Positive net monthly cash flow after obligations; ≤3 NSF/overdrafts in 90 days |
| **Utility/rent history** | On-time pattern in transactions (proxy for payment behavior) |
| **ITIN status** | Valid ITIN letter + gov ID + proof of address |
| **Fraud score** | Below vendor's high-risk threshold; device not linked to prior fraud |
| **First-payment-default flags** | No mismatch between stated & observed income >20% |

### Risk grades & pricing tiers

| Grade | Profile | Cash-flow score | Expected annual net charge-off | Max loan | APR (compliant) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Near-prime, strong stable cash flow, thick alt-file | Top tier | **8–10%** | $5,000 | ~29.99–35.99% |
| **B** | Near-prime/thin, adequate cash flow | Mid | **12–15%** | $3,500 | ~35.99% |
| **C** | Subprime, volatile cash flow | Low-pass | **18–25%** | $1,500 | ≤35.99% (state cap permitting) or **decline** |
| **D** | Fails cash-flow/fraud gates | Fail | — | — | **Decline** |

Benchmarks: near-prime NCO ~9–13% (Oportun 12% FY24), subprime ~15–25%, deep subprime ~35–45% (OppFi) — see appendix.

### Decline reasons (map to adverse-action codes)
Insufficient income; excessive DTI; insufficient/negative cash flow; unverifiable identity/ITIN; unverifiable income; excessive NSF/overdrafts; fraud-risk threshold; incomplete application; **OFAC hit** (handle per OFAC procedures, not a standard adverse-action). Every decline → **combined ECOA+FCRA adverse-action notice within 30 days.**

### Exposure & portfolio limits
- **Max single loan:** $5,000 (A only); most $1,500–$3,500.
- **Max exposure per borrower:** one active loan; no stacking.
- **Repeat-borrower policy:** on-time payoff → eligible for a higher tier / larger amount (build a graduation ladder; repeat performers are your best economics).
- **Concentration:** no single lead source, employer, or ZIP >X% of book (set at scale).

## 4. PHASE 4 — Loan Products, Pricing & Profitability

### Recommended launch product ("ITIN Builder Installment")
| Term | Value |
|---|---|
| **Loan amounts** | $1,000–$5,000 (avg ~$3,000–$4,000) |
| **Term** | 6–24 months, fully amortizing (12-month default) |
| **APR** | **35.99% headline** (stays under MLA 36% and most state caps; avoids Part 1041 trigger) |
| **Origination fee** | 0–5% where the state permits and it stays within all-in caps (adds yield without raising note rate; **excluded states: keep 0**) |

| **NSF/returned-payment fee** | ~$15 per state cap; **cap total attempts (Part 1041 two-strikes)** |

| **Risk-based pricing** | A ~30%, B ~36%, all ≤35.99% ceiling |

### Loss & return assumptions (industry-benchmarked)
- **Gross portfolio yield:** ~20–24% of original principal earned over a 12-mo amortizing 36% loan (declining balance), plus any origination fee.
- **Net charge-offs:** model **10–13% near-prime**, **18–25% subprime** (see appendix).
- **Target:** run an **A/B near-prime book** for a **net ROA in the high-single-digits / ROE in the mid-to-high teens** — consistent with Enova's ~17–20% ROE **[VERIFY exact figure]**. Deep-subprime-at-36% does **not** clear; either underwrite up or price up (only in permissive states, accepting reputational/regulatory risk).

## 7. PHASE 7 — Capital Model (27 apps, avg $4,000)

**Assumptions:** 12-month amortizing, **35.99% APR**, ~5% origination fee where permitted, in-house servicing. Charge-offs applied to principal. Illustrative — **not** a guarantee.

> **NOTE (post-lead-data, §0.5):** the $108k / 27-loan scenarios below are a *hypothetical* stress of the original brief. The **real** lead file contains only ~11 genuine loan inquiries, scattered across 8 states with none in an anchor state, so the *actually-fundable-by-you-now* set is ~**2 business loans** (NC $25k+, TN $5–10k) — not a $108k consumer book. Read the tables below as "what the model *would* look like at 27 concentrated consumer loans," and the §0.5 volume analysis as "what today's real volume supports" (answer: lead-gen, not direct lending).

| Scenario | # loans | Capital deployed | Gross yield + fee income (12 mo) | Net charge-offs | Opex (~$200/loan) | **Net (yr 1)** | **Return on deployed** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fund all 27** (blended A/B/C ~18% NCO) | 27 | $108,000 | ~$25,000 | ~$19,400 | ~$5,400 | **~+$200** | **~0.2%** (breakeven — proves the point) |
| **Fund 50%** (best 14, ~14% NCO) | 14 | $56,000 | ~$13,000 | ~$7,800 | ~$2,800 | **~+$2,400** | **~4.3%** |
| **Fund A-grade only** (~8 loans, ~9% NCO) | 8 | $32,000 | ~$7,500 | ~$2,900 | ~$1,600 | **~+$3,000** | **~9.4%** |

**Reading:** funding *everyone* at a compliant rate is ~breakeven; **funding fewer, better-underwritten loans is where the return lives.** The A-grade book (~9% ROA-equivalent yr 1) is the model to scale.

**Reserves & cash:** hold a **loan-loss reserve = expected lifetime NCO** (e.g., 12–18% of principal) plus **≥6 months opex** in operating cash. **Payback period** on A-grade ≈ the loan term (12 mo) as principal + yield recycle. **Loss scenarios:** stress NCO to 1.5× base (e.g., A-grade 9%→14%) and confirm you're still ≥breakeven before scaling.

> **Reality check:** the fastest *dollar-return* on your capital right now is likely **Lane 1/2 (lend to / participate with a licensed lender)** at a 12–20%+ contractual yield with none of the origination overhead — *while* you stand up Lane 4. Direct origination is a strategic build, not a yield play, in year one.

## 9. Risk Analysis (top risks & mitigations)

|---|---|---|
| **Lending to NY residents (criminal usury)** | Critical | Hard geo-block NY borrowers from direct origination; route to referral only |
