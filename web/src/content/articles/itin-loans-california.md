California has one of the largest ITIN-holder populations in the country, and lender interest in serving that population has grown with it. By 2020 the Hispanic population share in California surpassed that of White residents (41.1% vs. 36.18%), making Latinos the most populous racial group in the state. Even so, access to mainstream credit remains uneven. Systemic barriers threaten projected growth in Latino homeownership, and one major obstacle is the lack of mainstream mortgage financing for ITIN holders. This guide covers every loan type available, what California lenders actually require, and the fastest path to an approval.

## Does California actually allow ITIN loans, and are they legal?

*A question we hear often:* many readers assume their immigration status puts a mortgage or car loan completely out of reach. It doesn't.

You can buy a house in California with an ITIN number. Non-citizens, including undocumented individuals, are legally allowed to purchase property in the U.S., making homeownership accessible regardless of immigration status. On the privacy side, loan documents, including your ITIN number, are private information between you and the lender and are subject to federal privacy laws under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act; they are not shared with immigration agencies in the context of an ordinary mortgage transaction.

The demand side tells its own story. 2025 was a record-breaking year for Hispanic homeownership, with Latino households adding 441,000 new homeowners to reach a historic 10.2 million, the largest annual gain on record at a time when most other groups declined. The Urban Institute estimates that Latino households are poised to drive 70 percent of net homeownership gains through 2040, but for that projected growth to happen, the mortgage market needs to lower systemic barriers, one of which is a lack of mainstream financing for ITIN holders, a majority of whom are Latino.

## What types of ITIN loans are available in California?

When a loan application is accepted for a person who possesses a valid ITIN, the request is considered under normal lending criteria with some additional guidelines, and credit applications may be accepted for personal loans, auto loans, second mortgages, and credit cards.

## What do California ITIN lenders actually require?

*Readers frequently ask:* whether the requirements are so strict that qualifying is nearly impossible. In practice, the bar is firm but reachable if you prepare.

**Income and tax history.** A minimum down payment of 10%-20% is typically required, and you must file at least two years' worth of tax returns demonstrating a stable employment history under your ITIN number. Self-employed borrowers have a useful alternative: self-employed applicants can qualify using bank statements instead of tax returns, which is called a bank statement loan.

**Credit score or alternative credit.** In a review of several ITIN lenders, credit score requirements ranged from no required credit history at all to a 620-660 range. If you have no U.S. score yet, lenders will often accept alternatives. If you do not have any credit at all, lenders can look at non-traditional credit evaluation options, including rental payment history, utility bills, and phone bills.

**Debt-to-income ratio.** Most California ITIN lenders want your debt-to-income ratio under 55%. Keeping monthly obligations low relative to income is one of the fastest ways to improve your approval odds before you apply.

**Identification.** You will need your IRS ITIN assignment letter or card plus at least one government-issued photo ID from your country of origin, such as a passport or consular identification card.

## Which California lenders and credit unions approve ITIN loans?

*This one comes up a lot:* which specific institutions are actually willing to say yes?

You will not find ITIN home loans at many large, well-known mortgage companies. Credit unions, community development financial institutions (CDFIs), and small independent banks tend to issue the majority of ITIN mortgages. A few names stand out specifically in California:

**Strata Federal Credit Union (Bakersfield, CA):** Strata offers financing for residents who possess a valid ITIN, including 30-year fixed-rate mortgage terms with both purchase and refinance options available for primary residences.

**Redwood Credit Union (North Bay, CA):** Redwood Credit Union offers mortgages to members with an ITIN and required employment authorization documents, covering non-citizen homebuyers who do not have a Social Security number but do have an ITIN and possess a valid Permanent Resident Card, Work Authorization Card, or Work Visa. The minimum down payment for an ITIN mortgage loan at Redwood is 20%.

**Bay Federal Credit Union (Santa Cruz County, CA):** Bay Federal now offers ITIN holders access to all its mortgage products, including first and second mortgages and home equity lines of credit. Most applicants need a 20% down payment, and if you have no credit history, Bay Federal will accept four utility bills showing a year of on-time payment history.

**Santa Ana Federal Credit Union (Santa Ana, CA):** Santa Ana FCU is an ITIN-approved lender, and once you become a member you immediately qualify for low-interest rates on multiple loan products.

For statewide non-QM mortgage lenders, Griffin Funding, LBC Mortgage, Dream Home Financing, and Golden State Mortgage (Capital Home Mortgage California) all advertise active ITIN programs in California. CDFIs are nonprofit lenders certified by the U.S. Treasury that focus on underbanked communities and typically accept ITINs with lower interest rates than online lenders.

## How much will an ITIN mortgage cost me compared to a conventional loan?

Compared to mortgage types available to those with Social Security numbers, ITIN home loans can have interest rates between 0.5% and 2% higher than traditional mortgages. That gap adds up fast over a 30-year term, so rate shopping across multiple lenders isn't optional, it's necessary.

For reference, in June 2026, the average rate for a conventional 30-year loan in Los Angeles hovered around 6.8% according to Freddie Mac data. An ITIN borrower on the same property could expect something in the 7.3%-8.8% range depending on credit profile, down payment size, and lender type. Your down payment is the single most powerful lever you have: ITIN loan down payments tend to be higher than traditional mortgages because they represent a higher risk for the lender, but the higher the down payment, the better your interest rate.

On the secondary market side, the lack of a significant secondary market for ITIN loans is the largest barrier to expanding the market; government-sponsored enterprises Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac largely do not buy ITIN loans, and FHA does not insure mortgages if the borrower cannot prove legal U.S. residency. That is why ITIN mortgages are classified as non-QM loans and carried in lender portfolios, and why rates are higher.

## What is the step-by-step process to apply for a California ITIN loan?

The process is similar to a conventional loan application but with a few extra document checkpoints. Here is the sequence most California ITIN lenders follow:

1. **Pull your credit report.** Check what, if any, U.S. credit history exists under your ITIN at AnnualCreditReport.com before approaching a lender.
2. **Gather your documents.** You will need your ITIN letter or card, a valid passport or consular ID, two years of federal tax returns, 30 days of pay stubs or 12-24 months of bank statements for self-employed borrowers, proof of address, and evidence of your down payment funds.
3. **Start with credit unions and CDFIs.** Apply at a credit union or CDFI first, as their rates are best and they are most accommodating to ITIN applicants.
4. **Get pre-approved.** Gather all required documentation and review your financial situation, then submit your application to the lender for prequalification, which determines your eligibility and loan amount.
5. **Compare at least 2-3 lenders.** Terms vary widely. One lender might require 20% down while another accepts 10% for borrowers with a stronger credit profile.
6. **Make sure your down payment is seasoned.** Most lenders want to see your down payment funds sitting in a bank account for at least 60-90 days before closing so the source of those funds cannot be questioned.

## What properties can I finance with a California ITIN loan?

The types of properties you can buy with California ITIN loans depend on the lender, but in general you may be able to purchase single-family homes, condos, townhomes, multi-unit properties like duplexes, investment properties, and even vacation or second homes. ITIN DSCR mortgage loans in California also make real estate investing more accessible for borrowers without an SSN.

One practical note: condo, multi-family, and manufactured home financing can be more limited because fewer investors are willing to purchase those loan types on the secondary market. If you are targeting a condo complex or a manufactured home, confirm eligibility with the lender before you make an offer.

## How can I improve my chances of approval before applying?

*A question we hear often:* what can someone do right now, before they have enough for a down payment, to put themselves in the best position?

Three moves make the biggest difference.

**Build alternative credit now.** Rent payments, utility bills, and phone bills can all serve as credit references with ITIN-friendly lenders. Pay every bill on time and keep records of 12 consecutive months of payments for each account.

**Start a U.S. bank account and keep it active.** Lenders like to see that you have money saved. You will need your down payment in a bank account, and it should be sitting there for at least 2-3 months before you apply for your ITIN loan. Our [guide to opening a bank account with an ITIN](/articles/itin-bank-account) can help you get started.

**Use a secured credit card or credit-builder loan.** Applying for one or two ITIN-friendly credit products and paying them in full each month creates a formal credit record underwriters can actually review. See our guides on [ITIN credit cards](/itin-credit-card) and [credit-builder loans with an ITIN](/articles/itin-credit-builder-loan) for specific product recommendations.

According to the Urban Institute, an estimated 5,000 to 6,000 ITIN mortgages were made in 2023, but this could be up to 73,000 to 88,000 if market barriers were removed, meaning demand far outstrips supply and lenders are slowly catching up. Showing up prepared gives you a real advantage over other applicants.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I buy a house in California with an ITIN number?**
Yes. Neither federal law nor California state law requires citizenship or a Social Security number to purchase or own property. ITIN mortgage programs let you qualify using your IRS-issued tax ID, two years of tax returns, proof of income, and a down payment typically ranging from 10% to 25% depending on the lender.

**Which California lenders accept ITIN borrowers?**
Credit unions with strong Spanish-speaking member bases, such as Strata Federal Credit Union in Bakersfield, Redwood Credit Union in the North Bay, Bay Federal Credit Union in Santa Cruz County, and Santa Ana Federal Credit Union, all offer ITIN loan products. Non-QM mortgage companies like Griffin Funding, LBC Mortgage, and Dream Home Financing also serve California ITIN borrowers statewide.

**What credit score do I need for an ITIN loan in California?**
Most lenders look for a minimum score of 600-620. If you have no U.S. credit history at all, many California ITIN lenders will accept alternative credit references, such as 12 months of on-time rent payments, utility bills, phone bills, or car insurance premiums.

**How much do I need to put down on a California ITIN mortgage?**
Down payment requirements typically run from 10% to 25%, with 15%-20% being the most common benchmark. A larger down payment generally earns you a lower interest rate.

**Are ITIN loans in California legal and confidential?**
Yes on both counts. California law does not restrict property ownership based on immigration status. Loan documents and your ITIN are protected under the federal Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act and are not shared with immigration enforcement agencies in the normal course of a mortgage transaction.

**Can self-employed ITIN holders qualify for a California loan?**
Yes. Self-employed borrowers can substitute 12-24 months of bank statements for traditional W-2s or tax returns under bank statement loan programs that many California non-QM lenders actively offer.
