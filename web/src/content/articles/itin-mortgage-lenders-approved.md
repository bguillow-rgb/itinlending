## Why can't I just go to a big bank like Chase or Wells Fargo?

*A question we hear often:*

You cannot just walk into any big bank, like Chase or Wells Fargo, and expect them to offer [ITIN mortgages](/itin-mortgage). You need specialized lenders. The reason comes down to how mortgages are funded and sold. ITIN loans tend to have higher mortgage interest rates than conventional home loans because lenders consider ITIN loans to be higher risk. In addition to not being able to pull a credit history for the borrower, lenders typically need to keep ITIN mortgages on their own books, because these nonconforming loans are not government-backed and generally cannot be resold.

Big banks depend on selling most of their loans to Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac on the secondary market. Most traditional loans, like those backed by Fannie Mae or FHA, have strict requirements regarding residency status and SSN. Because ITIN holders do not fit these "qualified mortgage" boxes, ITIN loans fall under a category called Non-QM loans. That means only lenders willing to hold loans in their own portfolio or sell to non-QM investors will underwrite them.

The good news: while you will not find ITIN home loans at many mortgage companies with big brand names, you can find them at smaller financial institutions. Credit unions, community development financial institutions (CDFIs), and small, independent banks tend to issue the majority of ITIN mortgages. A handful of larger names have entered the space too, which we cover below.

## What are the ITIN mortgage rates I should actually expect?

*This one comes up a lot:*

ITIN mortgage rates are generally 1%-3% higher than standard loans. The reason? Lenders take on a bit more risk since ITIN borrowers often do not have long U.S. credit histories. In concrete terms: if a conventional 30-year fixed rate in mid-2026 is around 7%, an ITIN borrower with a solid profile might be quoted 8%-9%. Someone with a thinner credit file or smaller down payment could see 9.5% or higher.

ITIN mortgage rates are typically 1%-3% higher than conventional mortgage rates. The exact rate depends on your down payment, credit profile, loan amount, and the specific lender. Shopping multiple ITIN lenders and working with a knowledgeable broker can help you find the most competitive rate available.

The factors that move your rate the most are:

- **Down payment size:** A 20%-25% down payment consistently earns a lower rate than 10%-15%.
- **Credit score or alternative credit depth:** Scores above 700, or 4+ verified alternative tradelines, put you in the better rate tiers.
- **Loan amount:** Smaller loans close to or under $200,000 sometimes carry higher rates because fixed lender costs are spread across a smaller balance.
- **Lender type:** Credit unions and CDFIs sometimes price lower than non-QM wholesale investors, though their membership requirements can limit access.

Because rates for ITIN loans can be higher, shopping around is not just optional. It is a financial necessity. Getting quotes from at least three lenders before locking a rate is the single highest-leverage action an ITIN borrower can take.

## Do I need a U.S. credit score, or can I qualify with alternative credit?

*Readers frequently ask:*

This is one of the most misunderstood parts of the ITIN mortgage process. Most lenders prefer a U.S. credit score, but a meaningful number accept what the industry calls alternative tradelines. ITIN lenders use a manual underwriting process. Instead of just pulling a FICO score from a computer, a human underwriter looks at your full financial picture to assess risk.

At least 3 to 4 alternative credit references with 12 months of on-time payment history are typically required. Debt-to-income ratios are usually capped at 43%-50% depending on the lender. Acceptable alternative references include:

A history of on-time rent payments verified by your landlord, utility payment history from electric, gas, water, or phone companies, car insurance payment history, and other recurring payment obligations.

For borrowers who do have a U.S. credit score, most ITIN mortgage programs require a minimum credit score of around 620. Some lenders accept alternative credit documentation for borrowers without traditional credit histories. Higher credit scores typically qualify for lower down payments and better ITIN mortgage rates.

## What income documents do ITIN lenders accept?

Income documentation depends on your employment situation and the loan program. W-2 employees with ITIN-filed tax returns typically have the most straightforward path. Self-employed borrowers can choose between qualifying with tax returns or using a bank statement program, depending on which presents their income more accurately.

Here is how income documentation breaks down by borrower type:

| Borrower Type | Primary Income Docs Accepted |
|---|---|
| W-2 employee | Last 2 years of tax returns (Form 1040 filed with W-7) + 30 days of pay stubs |
| Self-employed | Last 2 years of tax returns OR 12-24 months of bank statements |
| Mixed income | Combination of 1099s, bank statements, and profit-and-loss statements |
| Foreign income | Some lenders (e.g., IDB Global FCU) accept last 2 years of tax returns from country of origin |

Lenders generally want to see 2 years of steady employment in the same line of work. If you changed jobs, it should be within the same industry.

For self-employed ITIN borrowers, the bank statement route often produces a higher qualifying income than tax returns, which frequently show large deductions. Some lenders offer bank statement loans where they calculate income based on 12-24 months of deposits into your business account, rather than tax returns.

One thing every ITIN borrower must confirm before applying: your ITIN must be current and not expired. Lenders will reject applications tied to expired or inactive ITINs. Always renew your ITIN before applying. If your ITIN needs renewing, see our step-by-step [ITIN renewal guide](https://itinlending.net/articles/itin-renewal).

## What makes an ITIN mortgage application get denied or delayed?

*A question we hear often:*

Several issues can make ITIN mortgage approval more difficult. These include an expired ITIN, inconsistent identity records, limited credit history, undocumented income, large unexplained deposits, high monthly debts, insufficient savings, or unclear residency documentation.

Most of what slows down closings is preventable. While every ITIN loan provider may tweak the checklist slightly, one thing never changes: accuracy matters. Inconsistent information or missing documents is the fastest way to slow down or lose approval for your ITIN home loan.

Here are the five most common avoidable problems, and how to fix them before you apply:

1. **Expired ITIN.** Renew through IRS Form W-7 at least 60 days before you plan to apply. Processing times at IRS Taxpayer Assistance Centers can run several weeks.
2. **Large unexplained bank deposits.** Lenders look for a clear paper trail showing where the money came from. Large, unexplained deposits often raise questions and can delay approval. Document any transfers before applying.
3. **Missing tax years.** Incomplete tax returns, missing W-2s, 1099s, or Form 1040 attachments can make your financial history look inconsistent. Double-check every page before submission.
4. **Applying with the wrong lender.** Approval can be harder if you apply for a loan program that does not accept ITIN borrowers. Call ahead to confirm a lender has an active ITIN program before submitting a full application.
5. **Insufficient reserves.** Beyond the down payment and closing costs, many ITIN lenders require 2-6 months of mortgage payments held in a verifiable account. Asset documentation confirms that you have funds for the down payment and closing costs, along with reserves to cover several months of mortgage payments if required.

## How long does the ITIN mortgage process actually take?

You can get approved for an ITIN loan in 24 hours. However, it can take 3-4 weeks to close your loan. More realistically, plan for 30-45 days from a complete application to closing if your documentation is well organized. Manual underwriting, which most ITIN loans require, adds review time compared to the automated underwriting systems used for conventional loans.

Underwriting review is the most detailed phase of the process. The underwriter evaluates income calculations, asset sourcing, credit documentation, and the property appraisal. Requests for additional documentation or clarification are common, particularly for bank statement programs or self-employment income. Prompt responses at this stage help keep the file moving.

Once the underwriter issues a conditional approval, clearing conditions quickly is what separates 30-day closings from 60-day closings. Keep your phone and email accessible and respond to document requests the same day.

## What types of properties can I buy with an ITIN mortgage?

ITIN mortgage loans can be used to finance various property types, including primary residences, vacation homes, and investment properties. The requirements vary significantly by property type, though:

- **Primary residence:** The most accessible option. Lowest down payment minimums (10%-15% at most lenders), best rates, and the widest range of programs.
- **Second home / vacation home:** Typically requires 20% down. Investment property and second home ARM loans include a maximum 80% loan-to-value.
- **Investment / rental property:** Higher down payment and stricter documentation. Some ITIN lenders also offer DSCR loans (debt service coverage ratio) that qualify you using the property's rental income rather than your personal income, which can be useful for investors.
- **New construction:** BuildBuyRefi is among the few lenders offering a one-time close ITIN construction loan. Most national lenders do not offer an ITIN construction loan at any LTV. BBR does.

You can use an ITIN loan to purchase, refinance, or cash out equity from your home. For more on refinancing an existing ITIN mortgage, see our guide on [how to refinance a mortgage with an ITIN](https://itinlending.net/articles/itin-mortgage-refinance).

## Should I use a mortgage broker or go directly to a lender?

*Readers frequently ask:*

For ITIN mortgages specifically, working with an experienced mortgage broker is often the smarter path. Here is why: mortgage brokers with experience in ITIN lending can be invaluable resources, as they have access to multiple lenders and can match you with the best program for your situation.

Home buyers with an ITIN number have difficulty finding a lender because conventional Fannie Mae guidelines do not permit applicants with an ITIN number. Most ITIN lenders are wholesale lenders who only work through a broker network. They create their own requirements for ITIN loans.

This means some of the most competitive ITIN mortgage programs, including options from A&D Mortgage and Angel Oak Loan Solutions, are only accessible through licensed brokers. A good broker already knows which investors have active ITIN programs, what their overlays are, and who is pricing competitively this week. That knowledge is worth the broker fee for most ITIN borrowers, because the rate difference between lenders can be 0.5%-1.5%. On a $300,000 loan over 30 years, that gap translates to tens of thousands of dollars.

Housing counseling agencies approved by HUD often maintain lists of ITIN-friendly lenders in your area and can provide free guidance. HUD-approved agencies are a good starting point if you want independent guidance before you engage with a lender or broker.

## Summary: how to pick the right ITIN mortgage lender

The right lender depends on four things: your state, your down payment, how you earn your income, and whether you have a U.S. credit score. Use the following decision logic:

- **Have a 620+ credit score and 15%-20% down, buying a primary home:** Start with Guild Mortgage or New American Funding. Both are nationally licensed, accept ITIN, and have Spanish-speaking support.
- **Self-employed with bank statement income:** Angel Oak Loan Solutions or A&D Mortgage (via a broker). Both have solid bank statement programs alongside ITIN options.
- **Want construction or a low-PMI ARM structure:** BuildBuyRefi is the only national lender publicly advertising an ITIN construction loan in all 50 states.
- **No U.S. credit score at all:** IDB Global Federal Credit Union accepts international credit reports and alternative tradelines. Some CDFIs and community banks in TX, CA, and FL have similar no-score paths.
- **Texas, California, or Florida with a credit union option:** Check local membership-based credit unions first. They typically offer lower fees and more personalized underwriting than national non-QM lenders.

Interest rates are usually higher than conventional mortgages, which makes lender comparison and preparation more important than with standard loan programs. Because ITIN mortgage terms are not standardized across lenders, borrowers who compare options and understand requirements upfront are more likely to secure better pricing and avoid avoidable delays.

No lender can guarantee approval before reviewing your full file. What you can control is arriving at the application with current documents, a verified ITIN, and at least two or three lender quotes to compare. That preparation is what separates ITIN borrowers who close in 30 days from those who spend months in limbo.
