If you've read our guide on [qualifying for an ITIN mortgage](/itin-mortgage-qualify), you already know the standard down payment for an ITIN home loan runs 10-20%. A partner lending program now offers a narrower alternative: a 3.5% down payment option built specifically for ITIN borrowers, modeled on the same minimum FHA loans use. It's a genuinely different number, but it's important to understand exactly what kind of loan this is, and isn't, before you count on it.

---

## Wait, does the FHA actually lend to ITIN holders now?

No, and this is worth being precise about. **A standard FHA loan, insured directly by HUD, requires the borrower to have a valid Social Security number and documented lawful residency or work-eligibility status.** An Individual Taxpayer Identification Number does not, by itself, satisfy that requirement. HUD Handbook 4000.1 sets the borrower-eligibility rules for FHA-insured loans, and an ITIN-only file does not meet them.

What exists instead is a **partner lending program** built to offer ITIN borrowers the same 3.5% minimum down payment FHA is known for, without being an actual HUD-insured FHA loan. It's a portfolio product, the lender holds the loan on its own books rather than selling it into an FHA-backed pool, structured to mirror FHA's low-down-payment threshold.

If that distinction sounds like a technicality, it isn't, it determines who backs the loan, what happens if the loan is sold or refinanced, and what recourse exists if something goes wrong. Ask the lender directly whether the loan is FHA-insured or a portfolio product before you apply; a program styled after FHA is not the same as an FHA loan.

---

## Why does a 3.5% down payment for ITIN borrowers matter?

Every other ITIN mortgage program we track, including standard non-QM options detailed in our guide on [how to qualify for an ITIN mortgage](/itin-mortgage-qualify), sits at 10-20% down for a primary residence. A small number of specialty lenders have advertised programs as low as 10%, but 20% remains the common baseline.

A 3.5% option changes the math meaningfully. On a $300,000 home, the difference between 20% down ($60,000) and 3.5% down ($10,500) is roughly $49,500, often the single biggest barrier standing between an ITIN holder and a signed contract. For borrowers with strong income and credit but limited cash reserves, a lower down-payment threshold can be the difference between buying this year and waiting several more years to save.

## Who is likely to qualify for the 3.5% down program?

Because this is a specialty partner product, not a universal FHA policy, eligibility is set by the lender and can include its own overlays on top of the base 3.5% structure. Based on how comparable ITIN non-QM programs typically underwrite, expect to need:

## What if I don't qualify for the 3.5% program?

If the partner program's overlays don't fit your file, that doesn't mean ITIN home financing is off the table, it means you're back to the broader ITIN mortgage market covered in our [complete guide to qualifying for an ITIN mortgage](/itin-mortgage-qualify), where 10-20% down remains the standard. It's also worth exploring [down payment assistance options for ITIN borrowers](/itin-down-payment-assistance), which can supplement whatever down payment threshold you're working with, whether that's 3.5% or 20%.

Rates on any ITIN mortgage, FHA-style or standard, tend to run 0.5-2 percentage points above conventional conforming loans, reflecting the non-QM/portfolio structure. That premium applies here too; a lower down payment does not mean a lower rate.
