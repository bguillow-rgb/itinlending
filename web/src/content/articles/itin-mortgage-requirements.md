Millions of people live, work, and pay taxes in the United States without a Social Security number, and every year, many of them wonder whether homeownership is within reach. The short answer is yes. An ITIN mortgage is a real, legal loan program specifically built for this situation, and the path to approval is more straightforward than most people expect once you know the steps.

This guide walks you through exactly how ITIN home loans work in 2026, what lenders look for, how rates and down payments compare to conventional loans, and how to move from application to closing. For the full program overview, see our [ITIN mortgage guide](/itin-mortgage).

## What Is an ITIN Mortgage and Who Qualifies?

An ITIN mortgage is a home loan that lets you use your Individual Taxpayer Identification Number, the nine-digit number the IRS issues to people who must file U.S. taxes but are not eligible for an SSN, in place of a Social Security number. According to research published by *National Mortgage Professional*, undocumented immigrants collectively pay an estimated **$11.74 billion in U.S. taxes per year**, and ITIN mortgage programs exist to give those tax-paying residents access to homeownership.

These loans are classified as **non-QM (non-qualified mortgage)** products because they fall outside the guidelines set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, which require a Social Security number. Instead, ITIN mortgages are held in lender portfolios or sold to private investors. That structure gives lenders more flexibility to evaluate your full financial picture rather than checking boxes on a government-backed checklist.

Who typically qualifies:
- Undocumented immigrants with a valid ITIN
- Non-resident aliens with U.S.-sourced income
- Certain visa holders (e.g., TN, E-2) who don't qualify for an SSN
- Foreign nationals who live and work in the U.S. part-time

Note that the IRS began expiring unused ITINs in 2017, so if you haven't filed taxes recently, you may need to renew your ITIN before applying for a mortgage.

## How Do ITIN Mortgage Rates and Down Payments Compare?

The honest trade-off with ITIN mortgages is cost and upfront cash. Because these are non-QM loans, lenders price in the added risk of holding them on their books rather than selling to Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac.

| Feature | ITIN Mortgage | Conventional Loan | FHA Loan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Minimum Down Payment | 10-20% (some as low as 3.5%) | 3-5% | 3.5% |
| Interest Rate Premium | +0.5% to +2.0% above conventional | Baseline | +0.1-0.5% above conventional |
| SSN Required | No | Yes | Yes |
| Credit Score Minimum | 620-640 (alt. credit accepted) | 620+ | 580+ |
| Government-Backed | No | Yes (Fannie/Freddie) | Yes (FHA) |
| Manual Underwriting | Yes | No | Partial |
| Loan Amounts | Up to $1.5M+ (varies by lender) | Conforming limits | FHA county limits |

According to multiple lender sources, ITIN mortgage rates typically run **0.5% to 2.0% higher** than comparable conventional loan rates, and most programs require **10% to 20% down**. A handful of specialized lenders now offer programs with as little as 3.5% down for borrowers with strong credit profiles.

For a self-employed borrower or one with limited U.S. credit history, a larger down payment, closer to 20%, meaningfully improves approval odds and can reduce the rate you're offered.

## What Documents Do You Need to Apply?

ITIN mortgage underwriting is manual, meaning a human reviews your full financial file rather than running it through an automated system. Getting your paperwork together before you contact a single lender is the single biggest thing you can do to speed up the process.

**Core documents most lenders require in 2026:**

- **Valid ITIN**, Your IRS assignment letter (CP565) or a tax return showing the number. If your ITIN has expired, renew via Form W-7 first.
- **Two years of federal tax returns**, Filed with your ITIN. W-2 employees have the clearest path; self-employed borrowers can use bank statements instead.
- **Proof of income**, Recent pay stubs (30 days), W-2s, or 12-24 months of personal/business bank statements.
- **Government-issued photo ID**, Passport, consular ID (Matrícula Consular), or other accepted document.
- **Bank statements**, Typically 2-3 months, showing your down payment funds and reserves.
- **Credit history**, Traditional U.S. credit report or 12 months of alternative references (rent, utilities, cell phone).
- **Proof of residence**, Utility bill, lease agreement, or other dated document.

Lenders flag large, unexplained bank deposits during underwriting, so document any gift funds separately. Many ITIN programs allow gift funds from family members to cover part of the down payment, just be prepared to show where the money came from.

## Where Can You Find ITIN Mortgage Lenders?

The biggest national banks, Chase, Wells Fargo, Bank of America, generally do not offer dedicated ITIN mortgage programs through standard channels. The most reliable sources are:

**1. Non-QM specialty lenders**, Companies like Acra Lending and similar portfolio lenders have built dedicated ITIN programs, sometimes with loan amounts up to $1 million and loan-to-value ratios up to 80%.

**2. Credit unions serving immigrant communities**, Member-owned and mission-driven, these institutions frequently offer the most competitive rates on ITIN mortgages. Because they keep loans in-house, they can be more flexible on qualification criteria. States with the deepest pools of ITIN lenders include California, Texas, Florida, New York, and Illinois.

**3. Community development financial institutions (CDFIs)**, CDFIs have a legal mandate to serve underbanked communities and are often willing to work with non-traditional credit profiles.

**4. Mortgage brokers with wholesale access**, A broker who specializes in non-QM lending can shop your file across multiple wholesale ITIN programs simultaneously, which saves significant time.

Historical performance data supports the case for these loans: a 2015 study found ITIN mortgage default rates at some credit unions ran as low as **0.82%-1.24%**, well below the national mortgage default average of **5.69%** at the time, suggesting ITIN borrowers are not the high-risk profile the higher rates might imply.

## What Is the Step-by-Step Process to Close an ITIN Mortgage?

Here's the full process from start to keys-in-hand:

**Step 1, Verify and renew your ITIN.** Confirm your ITIN is active and not expired. If you haven't filed a U.S. tax return using it in the past three years, file Form W-7 to renew it. Processing typically takes about seven weeks.

**Step 2, Pull your U.S. credit report.** Check whether you have a credit file by visiting AnnualCreditReport.com. If your file is thin or absent, spend three to six months building credit with an ITIN-accepted credit card before applying for a mortgage. Even a few months of on-time payments creates a workable file.

**Step 3, Save for your down payment and reserves.** Target at least 15-20% of your expected purchase price, plus 2-6 months of mortgage payments in reserves. Reserves signal stability to underwriters and can tip a borderline application toward approval.

**Step 4, Gather your document package.** Compile everything listed in the documents section above before speaking to any lender. Borrowers who arrive with a complete file close fastest.

**Step 5, Shop at least three ITIN lenders.** Rates on non-QM loans vary widely, far more than on conventional mortgages. Getting quotes from a non-QM specialty lender, a local credit union, and a mortgage broker gives you enough data to negotiate. A 0.5% rate difference on a $300,000 loan saves roughly $30,000 in interest over 30 years.

**Step 6, Apply for pre-approval.** Submit your full document package. Expect the underwriter to ask follow-up questions about your income, employment history, or source of funds. Respond quickly, delays in document turnaround are the most common cause of extended timelines.

**Step 7, Go under contract and complete appraisal.** Once pre-approved, make an offer. The lender will order an appraisal to confirm the property value supports the loan amount.

**Step 8, Close.** A well-prepared ITIN mortgage application closes in 30 to 45 days. At closing, you'll sign loan documents, pay closing costs (typically 2-5% of the loan amount), and receive your keys.

## Does an ITIN Mortgage Affect Your Immigration Status?

This is one of the most common concerns, and the answer is no. ITIN mortgage lenders are private financial institutions operating under banking regulations, not immigration enforcement agencies. They do not report your loan application, approval, or property purchase to U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), or any other immigration authority.

Owning a home in the United States does not grant immigration status, nor does it jeopardize an existing visa. The mortgage obligation remains valid regardless of changes to your immigration situation, if you were ever to leave the country, the property remains an asset you can sell, rent, or transfer.

That said, it's worth staying informed. In May 2026, an executive order directed federal regulators to review how financial institutions assess risk related to immigration status. As of the date of this article, no formal guidance restricting ITIN mortgage lending has been issued, and community lenders and credit unions serving immigrant borrowers have indicated they intend to continue their programs. If you have a pending application, consulting directly with your lender about the current timeline is the most practical step.
