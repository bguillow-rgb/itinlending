Can You Get a Home Equity Loan With an ITIN? (2026)

By Daniela Reyes · Published June 10, 2026

Aviso para anunciantes:ITIN Lending es un recurso independiente. Algunos enlaces en esta página son de socios que nos compensan, lo cual puede influir en qué productos mostramos y dónde. Esto no cambia nuestra guía editorial y nunca tiene costo para ti. Consulta nuestro aviso completo.

Respuesta rápida

Yes — ITIN holders can get a home equity loan or HELOC without a Social Security number. Select credit unions, CDFIs, and non-QM lenders accept ITINs. Expect a maximum LTV of 75–80%, two years of tax returns filed under your ITIN, and rates roughly 1–2% above conventional home equity products.

Already a homeowner — and now you want to put that equity to work. Maybe you need to renovate, consolidate higher-rate debt, or cover a major life expense. The question most lenders won’t answer clearly is this: can you tap your home equity if your only tax ID is an ITIN?

The short answer is yes, but your lender pool is smaller and the terms differ from what SSN holders encounter. Here is exactly what you need to know in 2026.

How does a home equity loan or HELOC actually work for ITIN holders?

A question we hear often:

A home equity loan gives you a lump sum secured by the equity you have built in your property, repaid at a fixed rate over a set term. A HELOC (home equity line of credit) works like a revolving credit line — you draw what you need, when you need it, during a set draw period, then repay the balance.

When it comes to financing tools, a home equity line of credit (HELOC) is one of the most flexible options for homeowners. Both products are available to ITIN holders, but through a narrower set of lenders than those offering purchase mortgages. The same non-QM lenders, portfolio banks, and credit unions that originate ITIN mortgages are the ones most likely to extend home equity products to ITIN borrowers.

Some credit unions offer home equity loans and HELOCs, and explicitly accept applications from individuals who have an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) as well as those with Social Security numbers. That written policy is exactly the kind of lender commitment you should look for when you shop.

Which lenders actually offer home equity loans to ITIN holders?

You won’t find ITIN home equity products at many big-brand mortgage companies. Credit unions, community development financial institutions (CDFIs), and small independent banks tend to be the primary source.

ITIN loan programs are offered by non-QM specialty lenders, portfolio banks, credit unions, and mortgage brokers who work with wholesale lenders. That landscape extends to home equity products: the same institutions that underwrote your purchase mortgage are the natural first stop when you want to tap equity.

ITIN mortgages are available nationwide, although availability varies by lender and region. Some states have more established ITIN lending markets than others. California, Texas, Florida, New York, and Illinois tend to offer a broader range of ITIN mortgage options. This pattern holds for home equity products as well.

What are the qualification requirements for an ITIN home equity loan?

This one comes up a lot:

The documentation checklist for an ITIN home equity loan closely mirrors what you would have submitted for your original ITIN purchase mortgage — with one critical addition: the lender will order a new appraisal to confirm current property value, which determines how much equity is actually available to borrow.

Typical requirements across most ITIN-friendly home equity lenders:

  • Valid ITIN issued by the IRS (must not be expired — see the IRS renewal rules)
  • Two years of U.S. tax returns filed under your ITIN — lenders typically require two years of tax returns filed with the ITIN, along with recent pay stubs and an employer verification letter
  • Proof of income: W-2s, 1099s, or 12 months of bank statements for self-employed borrowers — self-employed borrowers can choose between qualifying with tax returns or using a bank statement program, depending on which presents their income more accurately
  • Credit history: credit score requirements for ITIN loans can vary among lenders — a minimum score of 600 or 660 for some, while others may be more flexible, especially for borrowers with limited or no credit history
  • Sufficient equity: most ITIN lenders set a maximum combined loan-to-value (CLTV) of 75–80%, meaning your first mortgage balance plus the new home equity loan cannot exceed 75–80% of the home’s appraised value
  • Government-issued photo ID: passport, consular ID (matrícula consular), or national identity card
  • Proof of residency: utility bills or lease agreement showing your U.S. address

One item that surprises many borrowers: asset documentation confirms that you have funds for costs, along with reserves. Lenders look for a clear paper trail showing where the money came from. Large, unexplained deposits often raise questions and can delay approval.

How do ITIN home equity loan rates compare to conventional rates?

As of 2026, interest rates for ITIN mortgages are competitive and vary based on factors such as creditworthiness, down payment, and loan terms. You can expect interest rates for ITIN loans to be 1–3% higher than conventional rates depending upon your credit score and down payment. Home equity products follow the same premium logic.

To put this in concrete terms: if a conventional HELOC is offered at 8.00% APR in mid-2026, an ITIN HELOC from a portfolio lender might price at 9.00–10.50%. That spread narrows significantly when you have:

  • A credit score above 680
  • 30%+ equity in the property (lower CLTV = lower risk premium)
  • Two or more years of clean payment history on your existing mortgage
  • Documented, consistent income with minimal cash gaps

Home equity loans and HELOCs typically offer lower interest rates than credit cards or personal loans — even at the ITIN premium, this can still be the most cost-effective borrowing option for large expenses compared to an ITIN personal loan or ITIN credit card balance.

Can I do a cash-out refinance with an ITIN instead of a HELOC?

Readers frequently ask:

Yes — you can use an ITIN loan to purchase, refinance, or cash out equity from your home. A cash-out refinance replaces your existing mortgage with a new, larger loan and delivers the difference in cash at closing. It is structurally different from a HELOC or home equity loan, which sit as a second lien on top of your first mortgage.

Which path makes more sense for you often comes down to your current mortgage rate. If you locked in a rate below today’s market, a cash-out refinance would force you to give that up. A home equity loan or HELOC lets your low-rate first mortgage stay intact while you borrow against the equity separately.

For ITIN borrowers, both routes are available through non-QM and portfolio lenders. The documentation requirements are essentially the same — two years of tax returns, proof of income, credit evaluation, and a fresh appraisal. Closing timelines are similar too: you can get approved in 24 hours, but it typically takes 3–4 weeks to close.

One thing to watch: maximum loan-to-value for refinances is often capped at 80%, and cash-out options may be limited depending on the lender and program. Always ask whether cash-out is permitted before investing time in an application.

What about the 2026 executive order — does it affect my ability to borrow?

On May 19, 2026, President Trump signed an executive order that could affect immigrants’ ability to access banking and loans. The order directs federal financial regulators to issue guidance treating immigration status as a factor in evaluating financial risk, and flags ITINs as a potential area requiring enhanced scrutiny.

However, important context: the order stops short of requiring banks to actively collect immigration status or citizenship data from existing customers. The final order provides guidance rather than mandates. ITIN lending — including home equity products — remains legal, and lenders who specialize in this space have not signaled they are exiting the market.

Practical steps to protect your position:

  1. Document your financial history. Keep records of account statements, tax returns filed with your ITIN, and any loan documents in good standing.
  2. Do not close existing accounts or pay off loans early out of fear — a long, clean payment history is one of the strongest assets you have as an ITIN borrower.
  3. Work with lenders who have an established ITIN program and a stated policy of accepting ITIN identification.

Step-by-step: How to apply for an ITIN home equity loan

  1. Confirm your equity position. Use your most recent mortgage statement and a rough market estimate (Zillow, Redfin, or a quick call to a local agent) to determine how much equity you have. Subtract your mortgage balance from the estimated value — if the result is 25% or more of the home’s value, you are likely eligible.

  2. Check and strengthen your credit. Pull your credit report from all three bureaus using your ITIN. You can check your personal credit history and credit score with an ITIN with all three major credit reporting agencies — Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Each website allows you to submit a request for your credit report using an ITIN. Dispute any errors before applying. If your score is below 620, spend 6–12 months building it — see our ITIN credit-building guide for the fastest strategies.

  3. Gather your documents. Assemble two years of ITIN-filed tax returns, recent pay stubs or 12 months of bank statements, your government-issued photo ID, and proof of U.S. residency. Lenders also want to see your current mortgage statement.

  4. Find the right lender. Contact at least three ITIN-friendly lenders — ideally including a local credit union or CDFI and at least one non-QM lender. Ask each one directly: Do you offer home equity loans or HELOCs to borrowers with an ITIN and no SSN?

  5. Get pre-qualified, then formally apply. Pre-qualification uses a soft credit pull and gives you a rate range before you commit. Once you select a lender, the formal application triggers an appraisal and full underwriting review. The most preventable cause of denial is incomplete documentation. Missing or inconsistent paperwork forces underwriters to pause or decline files that might otherwise qualify. Working closely with a lender to confirm documentation requirements and submitting a complete file from the start significantly reduces this risk.

  6. Compare loan estimates side by side. Look at the APR (not just the interest rate), origination fees, appraisal costs, and whether a prepayment penalty applies. Lender comparison is non-negotiable. ITIN mortgage rates and fees vary dramatically between providers, and accepting the first offer costs you money.

Is the interest on an ITIN home equity loan tax-deductible?

This question matters especially for ITIN holders who already file U.S. taxes. The general rule under current IRS guidance: home equity loans and HELOCs follow the same deductibility rules. Through tax year 2025, interest is deductible only if the borrowed money was spent to “buy, build or substantially improve” the home securing the loan. You can’t deduct the interest if the proceeds were spent on other things, such as college tuition or a car.

To actually claim the deduction, you must itemize — if you take the standard deduction, you can’t deduct HELOC interest. To write off that interest, you have to itemize deductions on Schedule A. That’s why many homeowners don’t see a tax benefit from their HELOC, even if the interest technically qualifies under IRS rules.

This tax treatment applies the same way whether you use an SSN or an ITIN. Consult a tax professional who is familiar with ITIN filers for personalized guidance.

Bottom line

Owning a home with an ITIN means you have already cleared the hardest hurdle. Tapping that equity through a home equity loan or HELOC is a real, accessible option — you just need to direct your application to the right lenders. Credit unions, CDFIs, and non-QM portfolio lenders are your highest-probability targets in 2026. Bring two years of tax returns, document your income cleanly, and compare at least three offers before signing anything.

If you are still working on the home purchase step, visit our guide on how to buy a house with an ITIN number. If you want to borrow without putting your home on the line, a personal loan with an ITIN may be an alternative worth reviewing.

Preguntas frecuentes

Can I get a HELOC with an ITIN and no Social Security number?

Yes. Some credit unions and non-QM lenders explicitly accept ITINs for home equity lines of credit. You will need at least two years of U.S. tax returns filed with your ITIN, proof of stable income, and sufficient equity — typically at least 20–25% remaining after the draw. National banks rarely offer this program, so focus your search on credit unions, CDFIs, and portfolio lenders.

How much equity do I need to qualify for an ITIN home equity loan?

Most ITIN-friendly lenders cap the combined loan-to-value (CLTV) at 75–80%, meaning you generally need at least 20–25% equity in the property after accounting for your first mortgage balance plus the new home equity loan or HELOC amount.

Will applying for a home equity loan put my immigration status at risk?

ITIN lenders do not report loan applications to immigration authorities. A 2026 executive order directed regulators to flag certain ITIN activity as a potential red flag for enhanced bank scrutiny, but lenders are not required to share loan data with immigration enforcement. Keep copies of your tax returns, loan statements, and payment history as documentation of your financial standing.

Is a cash-out refinance a better option than a HELOC for ITIN holders?

It depends on your goals. A cash-out refinance replaces your entire mortgage at a new rate, which matters if current rates are higher than your existing rate. A HELOC or home equity loan keeps your first mortgage intact and is better when you need a lump sum or revolving access without resetting your primary loan terms. Both are available to ITIN holders through the right lenders.

What credit score do I need for an ITIN home equity loan?

Requirements vary by lender. Most ITIN-friendly lenders look for a credit score of at least 620–660. Some will consider alternative credit — documented on-time rent, utility, or phone payments — if your FICO score is limited. Building your credit profile before applying is the fastest route to better rates; see our guide on how to build credit with an ITIN.
