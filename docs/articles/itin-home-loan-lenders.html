ITIN Home Loan: Lenders, Rates & Requirements (2026)

Quick Answer

You can get an ITIN home loan through non-QM lenders, portfolio banks, credit unions, and CDFIs without a Social Security number. Most programs require 10%-20% down, two years of tax returns or 12-24 months of bank statements, a credit score of 600-660 (or alternative credit), and a valid, unexpired ITIN.

Not having a Social Security number does not shut you out of homeownership in the United States. Thousands of ITIN holders buy homes every year using programs that accept an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number in place of an SSN. The catch is that only certain banks, credit unions, and other lenders offer ITIN loans, and not all of them make that information easy to find on their websites, so you may need to do extra searching or make phone calls to confirm availability.

This guide pulls together what is scattered across dozens of lender sites into one verified reference: who approves ITIN home loans, what they actually require, what rates look like in 2026, and how to approach lenders in California, Texas, and Florida where demand is highest.

What exactly is an ITIN home loan, and how is it different from a regular mortgage?

An ITIN mortgage loan is a type of home loan designed for buyers who use an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number instead of a Social Security Number. The IRS issues these numbers to individuals who are required to have a U.S. taxpayer identification number but who do not have, and are not eligible to obtain, an SSN.

The loan itself works like any other mortgage: you borrow money to buy a home, make monthly principal and interest payments over 15-30 years, and build equity as you pay it down. What changes is the classification. Most traditional loans, like those backed by Fannie Mae or FHA, have strict requirements regarding residency status and SSN. Because ITIN holders don’t fit these “qualified mortgage” boxes, ITIN loans fall under a category called Non-QM loans, which means the banks or lenders keeping these loans on their own books have more flexibility.

ITIN loans tend to have higher mortgage interest rates than conventional home loans because lenders consider them higher risk. In addition to not being able to pull a credit history for the borrower, lenders typically need to keep ITIN mortgages on their own books because these nonconforming loans are not government-backed and generally cannot be resold. Lenders will charge a bit more to cover the risk of being stuck with the loan in case of borrower default.

That risk premium is real, but it is negotiable. Borrowers who bring a larger down payment, a solid alternative credit history, and two years of tax returns consistently qualify for better pricing than borrowers who arrive unprepared.

Which lenders actually approve ITIN home loans?

People apply at their regular bank, get turned away the moment they mention ITIN, and assume mortgages simply aren’t available to them.

You won’t find ITIN home loans at many mortgage companies with big brand names. Credit unions, community development financial institutions (CDFIs), and small, independent banks tend to issue the majority of ITIN mortgages. That said, a handful of larger or nationally scaled lenders have built verified ITIN programs:

Flagstar Bank offers a fixed-rate conventional mortgage that you can apply for using your Individual Tax Identification Number instead of an SSN, available for buying a home or refinancing, with U.S. citizenship not required. Flagstar is licensed to originate in all 50 states, and its assistance programs offer eligible first-time buyers in specific areas grants of up to $10,000 for down payment and closing costs, with first-generation home buyers potentially qualifying for $25,000 in assistance.

Guild Mortgage, a non-bank lender based in San Diego available in 49 states, offers non-QM loans for homebuyers who can’t meet typical credit and income requirements, including ITIN loans, bank statement loans, and non-warrantable condo loans. Its “Complete Rate” program uses alternative creditworthiness reporting, including rental payment history, which gives borrowers with no traditional credit score a chance at a better interest rate.

BuildBuyRefi (powered by The Federal Savings Bank, NMLS #411500) is one of the more transparent national programs. ITIN purchase loans finance owner-occupied primary residences with as little as approximately 10.01% down on loan amounts up to $500,000, structured as a 5/1 or 7/1 adjustable-rate mortgage amortized over 30 years. No private mortgage insurance is required at any LTV tier. The program also offers ITIN cash-out refinance to 75% LTV and one-time close construction loans.

CDFIs and mission-driven credit unions are often the best fit for borrowers with thin credit profiles or lower incomes. Community Development Financial Institutions, known as CDFIs, are mission-driven lenders that focus on underserved populations and frequently offer ITIN mortgages with competitive terms. HOPE Credit Union serves families across the Deep South. IDB Global Federal Credit Union, for example, requires no U.S. credit score and may use rent history, utility bills, or international credit reports if required.

What down payment do I need for an ITIN mortgage in 2026?

This is the number that surprises most first-time ITIN homebuyers, especially if they’ve heard about FHA loans allowing 3.5% down. The down payment requirement for ITIN mortgages tends to be considerably higher than many traditional loan programs, between 15% and 25%. Compared to FHA loans with a low 3.5% down payment requirement, the difference can feel steep. The higher down payment helps lenders offering ITIN mortgages offset risk since there’s no government backing.

In practice, the range is wide:

Down PaymentWhat It Gets You

10%-15%Available from most national non-QM lenders with strong credit and income docs
15%-20%The most common requirement; opens up a broader lender pool
20%-25%Required by some portfolio banks and credit unions; often needed with lower credit scores

Most ITIN mortgage programs require a down payment of 10-25%, with 15%-20% being the most common requirement. A larger down payment can improve your approval chances and reduce your interest rate. Gift funds from a family member are accepted by many lenders, but the money typically needs to be in your bank account for 30-60 days before you apply so it clears sourcing rules.

If saving 20% feels out of reach right now, a few strategies are worth considering. First, look for CDFI programs in your metro area, which sometimes have lower down payment requirements for income-qualified borrowers. Second, check down payment assistance programs in your state; many do not ask about immigration status if you pay taxes with an ITIN. Third, consider starting with a credit builder loan or secured credit card to build a credit score that qualifies you for the lowest available down payment tier.

What credit score is required, and what if I have none?

Whether having no U.S. credit history at all is an automatic disqualifier.

It is not, but it does narrow your lender options. In Yahoo Finance’s review of several ITIN lenders, credit score requirements ranged from no required credit to a 620-660 range, with down payment requirements of 15%-25%. Here is what borrowers actually encounter across the lender landscape:

  • No U.S. credit score: Accepted by IDB Global FCU (uses international credit or rent history), HOPE Credit Union, BuildBuyRefi (no-score path with 3 acceptable tradelines), and some CDFIs.
  • 580-600 minimum: Dream Home Financing, some community lenders.
  • 620 minimum: Guild Mortgage (conventional programs), many non-QM lenders.
  • 640 minimum: BuildBuyRefi (scored path).
  • 660 minimum: JVM Lending, some stricter non-QM programs.

If you have no U.S. credit score, you will need to document alternative credit. This includes a history of on-time rent payments verified by your landlord, utility payment history from electric, gas, water, or phone companies, car insurance payment history, and other recurring payment obligations. At least 3-4 alternative credit references with 12 months of on-time payment history are typically required.

If building a traditional credit score is your goal before applying, our guide to how to build credit with an ITIN number covers the fastest paths, including secured cards and credit builder loans that report to all three bureaus.

What interest rate will I actually pay on an ITIN home loan?

Rate ranges for ITIN mortgages in 2026 depend on which type of lender you use and how strong your financial profile is. The premium over conventional rates is real, but it is not uniform.

ITIN mortgage rates are typically 1-3% higher than conventional mortgage rates. The exact rate depends on your down payment, credit profile, loan amount, and the specific lender. Some sources, including McGowan Mortgages, put the typical range at 0.5%-2% higher than traditional mortgages. The spread varies because non-QM and portfolio lenders price differently from each other.

To put real numbers on it: finding a lender is often the hardest part because rates vary wildly between lenders. One lender might quote 8% while another quotes 9.5% for the same borrower profile. Shopping at least three lenders before committing is not optional for ITIN borrowers. It is one of the highest-return actions you can take.

How do lenders verify income without W-2s?

Many ITIN holders are self-employed, work multiple gigs, or receive income that doesn’t map neatly onto a W-2. ITIN lenders have specifically built alternatives for this situation.

There are two main income documentation paths.

Tax return path: You submit the last two years of signed federal tax returns filed under your ITIN. The lender uses your net income (after deductions) to calculate how much mortgage you qualify for. This is the most straightforward path for W-2 employees and for self-employed borrowers whose tax returns show strong net income.

Bank statement path: Bank statement programs allow ITIN borrowers to qualify using 12-24 months of deposits rather than tax returns. These programs are particularly useful for self-employed borrowers whose tax returns don’t reflect their true income because of legitimate business deductions. The lender averages your monthly deposits and uses a percentage of that figure as qualifying income. Most lenders require 12-24 months of statements.

Debt-to-income ratios are usually capped at 43%-50% depending on the lender, consistent with the broader non-QM market. Keeping your monthly debts (credit cards, car loans, personal loans) as low as possible before applying directly expands the mortgage amount you can qualify for. Our personal loan guide for ITIN holders covers how to manage existing debt strategically.

What documents do I actually need to apply?

Incomplete documentation is the single most common reason ITIN mortgage applications stall or get denied. Assembling everything before you contact a lender puts you in the strongest possible position.

Identity documents:

  • Valid ITIN assignment letter or card (must be unexpired; if yours has lapsed, see our ITIN renewal guide)
  • Government-issued photo ID: passport, Matricula Consular, foreign driver’s license, or U.S. driver’s license

Income and employment:

  • Last two years of signed federal tax returns (1040 with all schedules)
  • W-2s or 1099s if applicable
  • OR 12-24 months of personal and/or business bank statements (bank statement program)
  • Profit-and-loss statement if self-employed

Credit documentation (if no U.S. credit score):

  • 12-month payment history from landlord (letter or statement)
  • 12-month utility, phone, or insurance payment records
  • International credit report if available
  • At least 3-4 alternative tradeline references

Assets:

  • 2-3 months of bank statements showing down payment funds
  • Down payment funds must typically be seasoned (in your account 30-60 days)
  • Gift letter from family member if using gifted funds

Property:

  • Executed purchase contract once under contract
  • Property appraisal (ordered by lender)

A common mistake: using an expired ITIN. Lenders will reject applications tied to expired or inactive ITINs. Always renew your ITIN before applying.

Are ITIN home loans available in Texas, California, and Florida?

Whether ITIN lending is concentrated in specific states or genuinely available everywhere.

ITIN mortgages are legal in all 50 states, but availability varies by lender and region. Some states have more established ITIN lending markets than others, particularly those with larger immigrant populations. California, Texas, Florida, New York, and Illinois tend to offer a broader range of ITIN mortgage options.

Migration Policy Institute and demographic research consistently identify five states as the highest-demand markets for ITIN home financing: Texas, California, Florida, New Jersey, and Illinois. Together these states account for the majority of ITIN borrower applications nationwide.

Texas: The largest concentration of ITIN-eligible homebuyers is found here, and multiple lenders operate actively in the market. Simmons Bank’s ITIN Affordable Advantage Mortgage program is offered specifically for properties in Dallas, Fort Worth, Houston, Austin, San Antonio, Corpus Christi, and surrounding metro areas under its Community Reinvestment Act obligations. First Financial Bank (ffin.com) also operates a community ITIN mortgage program in designated Texas assessment areas, with loan amounts up to $350,000. National non-QM lenders like Dream Home Financing and BuildBuyRefi cover Texas with no state-specific exclusions.

California: ITIN loans are available throughout California, where there are many ITIN cardholders who wish to purchase a home. Down payment requirements in California for an ITIN loan are slightly different, with the smallest down payment possible being 20% from most lenders, though some specialty programs offer lower options. For income-qualified first-time buyers, CalHFA offers FHA loan programs consisting of 30-year fixed mortgages that can be combined with programs for down payment and closing cost assistance. JVM Lending and Sierra Lending both specialize in California ITIN borrowers, and Strata Federal Credit Union (Kern County) accepts ITIN for home loans. Flagstar Bank has branches in California and its ITIN program is available statewide.

Florida: Foundation Mortgage Corporation (Miami Beach, NMLS #5057) operates an ITIN program that covers loan amounts up to $2.5 million for full-doc primary residence and investment properties, and includes eligible property types such as single-family homes, 2-4 unit buildings, condos, and non-warrantable condos. Flagstar is licensed in Florida and its ITIN program extends statewide. National non-QM lenders, including ACC Mortgage, Dream Home Financing, and NonPrimeLenders.com, all cover Florida.

What is the ITIN mortgage application process, step by step?

The timeline from first contact to keys in hand is typically 30-45 days from the point you submit a complete file. Most delays come from documentation gaps discovered after application, not from underwriting complexity.

Step 1: Pre-qualify (1-3 days). Contact 2-3 lenders who explicitly offer ITIN programs. Discuss your ITIN status, income sources, approximate credit profile, and target home price. Get preliminary rate and down payment estimates from each.

Step 2: Assemble your documents (1-2 weeks). Gather everything from the document checklist above before submitting a formal application. Lenders experienced with ITIN loans can move quickly once they have a clean file. Complete and well-organized files are critical at this stage, as missing documents are the most common cause of extended ITIN mortgage timelines.

Step 3: Formal application and appraisal order. Submit the full application package. The lender reviews the file for completeness, pulls credit if traditional credit is being used, and orders the property appraisal. Initial disclosures are issued outlining loan terms, estimated closing costs, and other required information.

Step 4: Underwriting. The underwriter evaluates income calculations, asset sourcing, credit documentation, and the property appraisal. Requests for additional documentation or clarification are common, particularly for bank statement programs or self-employment income. Respond to any requests promptly.

Step 5: Conditional approval. The lender approves the loan subject to a specific list of remaining items. Satisfy each condition as quickly as possible.

Step 6: Clear to close and closing. Once all conditions are satisfied, the lender issues a clear-to-close, the closing appointment is scheduled, and funds for the down payment and closing costs are prepared. At closing, final documents are signed, funds are transferred, and ownership of the property is completed.

Closing costs on ITIN mortgages mirror those on conventional loans: roughly 3%-5% of the loan amount, covering appraisal, title, origination fees, and prepaid items such as homeowner’s insurance and property taxes.

What are the biggest mistakes ITIN mortgage applicants make?

After reviewing patterns across multiple ITIN lender sources, four mistakes account for the majority of denials and delays.

1. Applying with an expired ITIN. Lenders cannot legally process your application with an inactive ITIN. If your ITIN has not been used to file taxes in three consecutive years or falls into an expiring batch, renew it well before applying. See our ITIN renewal guide for the current process.

2. Inconsistent tax returns. Missing schedules, years with gaps in filing, or income figures that don’t match bank deposits are red flags for underwriters. Missing W-2s, 1099s, or Form 1040 attachments can make your financial history look inconsistent. Double-check every page before submission.

3. Depositing a large cash sum right before applying. Down payment funds need to be sourced and seasoned. You need to show you have the funds for the down payment and closing costs. The money usually needs to be “seasoned,” meaning it has been sitting in your bank account for at least 30-60 days. Do not deposit a large amount of cash the day before you apply. It raises red flags.

4. Applying at only one lender. Because ITIN mortgage rates and requirements vary more than conventional loans, shopping multiple ITIN lenders and working with a knowledgeable broker can help you find the most competitive rate available. A rate difference of even 1% on a $300,000 mortgage translates to roughly $60,000 in additional interest over 30 years.

How can I lower my ITIN mortgage rate over time?

Your rate at closing is not permanent. There are two primary ways to reduce your cost over the life of the loan.

Refinance once you have an SSN or improved credit. Some ITIN holders eventually obtain a Social Security number, qualify for lawful permanent residency, or simply build enough U.S. credit history to refinance into a conventional loan at a significantly lower rate. You can expect interest rates for ITIN loans to be 1%-3% higher than conventional rates depending upon your credit score and down payment. Closing that gap through a refinance can save tens of thousands of dollars. Our guide to refinancing a mortgage with an ITIN number walks through the exact scenarios.

Build home equity quickly. A larger down payment at closing, or extra principal payments afterward, lowers your loan-to-value ratio. Once your LTV drops below 80%, you may have options to refinance into better-priced programs, depending on your lender.

In the meantime, also consider whether tapping home equity later makes sense. If you build meaningful equity and your lender allows it, a home equity loan with an ITIN or HELOC with an ITIN can be a lower-cost borrowing option than personal loans or credit cards.

Will applying for a mortgage affect my immigration status?

This concern stops many otherwise-qualified ITIN holders from ever starting the process.

ITIN mortgage lenders are not obligated to report your information to immigration authorities. The process is completely separate from the immigration system. Your mortgage application does not trigger any verification of status with ICE or USCIS.

Having an ITIN number does empower these individuals to pay taxes and participate in the U.S. economy through products like mortgages, but these numbers don’t convey citizenship or work authorization in either direction. The ITIN is purely a tax-filing tool.

Lenders are regulated by federal and state banking authorities (CFPB, FDIC, state mortgage regulators) and operate under fair-lending laws including the Equal Credit Opportunity Act. According to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, borrowers need to meet standards set by the individual lender, and those standards are credit-based, not immigration-enforcement-based.
