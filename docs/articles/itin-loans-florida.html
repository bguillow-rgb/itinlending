ITIN Loans in Florida: Best Options & How to Qualify (2026)

Quick Answer

Yes, ITIN holders can get loans in Florida without a Social Security number. Non-QM mortgage lenders, credit unions, and specialty auto and personal loan lenders all serve Florida ITIN borrowers. Mortgages typically require 10%-20% down, a minimum credit score near 620, and two years of tax returns filed with your ITIN.

Florida is one of the top three states in the country for ITIN lending activity, alongside Texas and California. If you pay taxes with an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number and want to buy a home, finance a car, or borrow for personal needs in the Sunshine State, you have more options than most people realize. This guide walks through every major ITIN loan category available in Florida, what lenders look for, what rates to expect, and exactly how to apply.

Is it actually possible to get a loan in Florida with an ITIN instead of an SSN?

A question we hear often: Yes, and the answer is simpler than you might expect. ITIN loans allow homebuyers without a Social Security number to qualify for a mortgage using an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number instead. The same applies to auto and personal loans. No federal statute requires a Social Security number to take out a loan. Whether a lender accepts an ITIN is a business decision, not a legal barrier.

ITIN loans are fully legal, fully legitimate, and increasingly common in Florida’s large immigrant buyer market. Research consistently identifies Florida as one of the five highest-demand states for ITIN home financing, and together these top states account for the majority of ITIN borrower applications nationwide.

One data point worth sitting with: a 2024 report from the Urban Institute found that there are approximately 5.25 million ITIN holders currently residing in the U.S., yet fewer than 6,000 ITIN mortgages were closed in 2023. That gap means a large share of qualified borrowers simply haven’t found the right lender yet. This guide is meant to close that gap for Florida residents.

What do Florida lenders actually require for an ITIN mortgage?

Readers frequently ask: The requirements are consistent across most Florida ITIN programs, with small variations by lender.

These loans typically require a 10%-20% down payment, verifiable income through tax returns or bank statements, a minimum credit profile or alternative credit, and documented residency. Most programs in Florida specifically ask for:

  • A valid ITIN issued by the IRS (your CP565 assignment letter or ITIN card)
  • Two years of federal tax returns filed under your ITIN
  • Proof of stable income (pay stubs, bank statements, or profit-and-loss statements for the self-employed)
  • Government-issued photo ID such as a passport or consular ID (Matrícula Consular)
  • Down payment funds that can be documented and, in many cases, seasoned

Most ITIN mortgage loans in Florida require a minimum credit score between 620 and 660, though some lenders may accept alternative forms of credit verification if you have limited U.S. credit history. No FICO score at all? Lenders often accept 12 months of documented on-time rent payments, utility bills, or a credit report from your country of origin. For more on building that foundation, see our guide on how to build credit with an ITIN number.

One requirement that catches people off guard is cash reserves. Most ITIN borrowers need to show six to twelve months’ worth of mortgage payments, after closing, held in liquid assets such as savings, checking, or investment accounts. Start saving early.

How much does an ITIN mortgage in Florida actually cost?

This one comes up a lot: The rate premium over a conventional loan is real, but manageable if you prepare.

ITIN mortgage rates are usually 0.5%-2% higher than conventional loan rates, and down payment requirements are higher, typically 10%-20%. In practice for Florida in 2026, ITIN mortgage rates generally range from the mid-7s to low-9s depending on your credit profile, down payment, loan amount, and property type. For context, conventional 30-year fixed rates currently hover between 6.37% and 6.50%, so the typical ITIN premium adds roughly one to two percentage points.

Why the premium? ITIN mortgage loans are classified as non-QM loans because they do not meet the lending criteria set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, and as a result they are usually held in lender portfolios or sold to private investors rather than to government-sponsored enterprises. Lenders offset that illiquidity by charging a higher rate and requiring more equity upfront.

You can close the gap a few ways: put down 20% or more (which also eliminates private mortgage insurance on many programs), keep your credit score above 680, provide 24 months of clean tax returns showing consistent income, and compare at least three lenders before signing. Our guide to ITIN mortgage rates goes deeper on the levers you can pull.

Which types of lenders offer ITIN loans in Florida?

ITIN mortgages are most commonly offered by non-QM specialty lenders, credit unions serving immigrant communities, portfolio banks, and mortgage brokers with access to wholesale lenders. Large national banks generally do not offer ITIN mortgage programs.

In Florida specifically, the market is well developed. A few lender categories worth knowing:

Non-QM specialty lenders process the most ITIN volume. They have underwriters who handle alternative documentation routinely, which means fewer delays. Non-QM lenders typically charge higher ITIN mortgage rates and fees to account for the added complexity and risk of these loans, but in exchange they offer broader approval flexibility.

Credit unions can offer competitive pricing. Credit unions frequently offer competitive ITIN mortgage programs, particularly those serving regions with large immigrant communities. Because they are member-owned, they may offer lower fees and more personalized service than larger institutions. The trade-off is that membership eligibility is tied to geography or affiliation, so not every Florida credit union will be accessible to every applicant.

Mortgage brokers are worth considering because a single broker can submit your file to multiple wholesale lenders at once. Only certain banks, credit unions, and other lenders offer ITIN loans, so you may need to do more shopping around than an applicant would for a traditional mortgage, and not all lenders that offer ITIN loans make that information easy to find. A broker who knows the non-QM space can shortcut that search considerably. See our roundup of ITIN mortgage lenders that actually approve for specific names.

What about ITIN auto loans in Florida?

Florida has one of the strongest markets for ITIN auto financing in the country. Many franchise dealerships, particularly in Miami, Tampa, and Orlando, have relationships with specialty lenders that accept ITIN applications. Some large dealer groups even advertise this directly to Spanish-speaking buyers.

For auto loans, lenders focus primarily on your income, time at your current address, and the loan-to-value ratio on the vehicle. A down payment of 10%-20% of the vehicle price improves approval odds and lowers your rate. Some credit unions serving Florida’s immigrant communities, including ITIN-specific programs at select federal credit unions, offer auto loans to ITIN members at rates well below what dealer-arranged financing typically runs.

For a full breakdown of the auto loan process, our guide on how to get a car loan with an ITIN number covers lender types, documentation, and negotiation strategy in detail.

Can I get a personal loan in Florida with an ITIN?

Readers frequently ask: Yes, though the options are narrower than for mortgages or auto loans.

Several categories of lenders work with ITIN holders, including many local and regional credit unions, especially in states with large immigrant populations. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs) are nonprofit lenders certified by the U.S. Treasury that focus on underbanked communities. They typically accept ITINs and have lower interest rates than online lenders.

For unsecured personal loans without an established U.S. credit score, expect rates between 15%-36% APR from fintech lenders. A $5,000 loan at 24% APR over 36 months carries monthly payments around $196 and total interest of about $2,068, while a credit union or CDFI at 14% APR would cut that to about $171 per month and $1,156 total interest. That difference adds up, so joining a credit union before you need the loan is often worth the effort.

Why does Florida stand out as a state for ITIN borrowers?

A few structural factors make Florida more accessible for ITIN holders than most other states.

The immigrant community is large and well-established. Florida is home to one of the largest immigrant communities in the United States, and Central Florida specifically has seen enormous growth in Caribbean, Latin American, and African immigrant populations over the past decade. That demographic reality means more lenders have built dedicated ITIN programs here.

Florida also has no state income tax. Florida has zero state income tax, which means more of your income goes toward building wealth, including your mortgage payment and property equity. That helps debt-to-income ratios, which are a core part of ITIN mortgage underwriting.

The homeownership rate in Florida was 66.2% in 2025, slightly above the national average, and the state’s large single-family housing supply gives buyers a wide range of price points to work with. Eligible properties under most ITIN programs include single-family homes, townhouses, condominiums, and 2-4 unit properties.

Lender competition in Florida is high, too. More lenders competing for ITIN business generally means better terms for borrowers who shop around.

What is the step-by-step process to get an ITIN loan in Florida?

  1. Confirm your ITIN is current. An expired ITIN will stop an application immediately. If renewal is needed, complete IRS Form W-7 well before you apply. Our ITIN renewal guide walks through the process.
  2. Check your credit. Pull your free report at AnnualCreditReport.com to see what credit history exists under your ITIN. If it’s thin, spend 6-12 months building alternative tradelines before applying.
  3. Gather documents. Collect your ITIN assignment letter, last two years of federal tax returns, two months of bank statements, proof of income (pay stubs or profit-and-loss statements), and your government-issued photo ID.
  4. Get pre-qualified with multiple lenders. Contact at least two or three non-QM lenders or a broker who specializes in ITIN loans. Pre-qualification lets you compare rates and terms without a hard credit pull in most cases.
  5. Compare loan estimates carefully. Look at the APR, not just the rate. Fees, points, and closing costs vary a lot between ITIN lenders.
  6. Submit a full application and complete underwriting. Most delays come from missing paperwork, appraisal issues, or follow-up underwriting requests. A file that is complete from the start usually moves faster and creates fewer surprises before closing.
  7. Close and move in. Once conditions are cleared, you sign closing documents and receive your keys or funds.
