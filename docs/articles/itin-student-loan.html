Can You Get a Student Loan With an ITIN? (2026 Guide)

By Editorial Team · Loans, Mortgages & Credit · Published June 11, 2026

Advertiser disclosure:ITIN Lending is an independent resource. Some links on this page are from partners who compensate us, which may affect which products we feature and where. This does not change our editorial guidance and is never a cost to you. See our full disclosure.

Quick Answer

Yes, ITIN holders can get private student loans, several lenders accept an ITIN instead of an SSN. Federal loans (FAFSA) require a Social Security number and are off-limits. Your best options are ITIN-accepting private lenders, CDFIs, and state-level aid programs available in California, Texas, New York, and other states.

If you hold an ITIN and are trying to pay for college, the single most important thing to understand upfront is this: the federal student loan system is closed to you, but the private lending and state aid markets are not. This guide walks through exactly where ITIN holders stand in 2026, which lenders will actually say yes, and how to put together the strongest possible application.

Why can’t I just fill out the FAFSA with my ITIN?

A question we hear often:

Federal student loans are issued by the U.S. federal government and are intended for U.S. citizens, permanent residents, and eligible non-citizens, and the government uses Social Security numbers to verify that borrowers fall into one of those categories. An ITIN is a tax-processing number, not a residency or citizenship credential, so it cannot substitute for an SSN on the FAFSA.

DACA recipients and undocumented students are not eligible for federal student loans, which rules out Pell Grants, Stafford Loans, and federal PLUS loans for this population. This is a statutory restriction, not a policy choice individual schools can override.

The good news is that federal loans represent only one slice of how students pay for college. Private lenders, CDFIs, and a growing number of state governments have all stepped into the gap.

So can I actually get a private student loan with my ITIN?

Yes, but the lender pool is narrower than it is for SSN holders. When a private lender underwrites a student loan, they typically check your credit score, and most use your SSN to pull that report from the three major bureaus, which is why the majority of private student lenders require an SSN. That said, a meaningful minority of lenders have built underwriting systems that work with ITINs, alternative credit data, or cosigners.

Some lenders accept ITINs instead of Social Security numbers, and a few offer loans without a cosigner. The key is knowing which categories of lenders to approach and what documentation will make your file competitive.

What lenders specifically work with ITIN holders for student loans?

This one comes up a lot:

MPOWER Financing is the most cited cosigner-free option for ITIN holders. MPOWER offers private student loans to F-1 visa holders without requiring a U.S. credit history or cosigner, helping manage school expenses while keeping your financial record clean, and MPOWER reports payments to major credit bureaus, so consistent repayment can support your credit profile over time. DACA borrowers with ITINs are also eligible.

Ascent Funding is another option that has worked with ITIN borrowers. Ascent’s published APRs are effective as of June 2026 and reflect automatic payment discounts, with a 0.5% ACH discount for credit-based loans submitted on or after June 1, 2025. Ascent generally requires either an SSN or a creditworthy cosigner, but its outcomes-based loan product evaluates borrowers partly on school and program, meaning a student at a high-earning-potential program may qualify without a traditional credit score.

Credit unions in high-immigrant-population states, particularly in California, Texas, Florida, Illinois, and New York, often have the most flexible ITIN policies and the lowest rates. Local credit unions and CDFIs often have the most relaxed ITIN policies and the lowest rates; if you can’t qualify locally, fintech lenders focused on ITIN and immigrant borrowers are another avenue.

When you approach any lender, ask two questions immediately: (1) Do you accept an ITIN in place of an SSN? and (2) Do you report loan payments to all three credit bureaus under the ITIN? A yes to both is ideal, the second question matters because on-time payments that don’t get reported do nothing for your credit file.

Does my state offer any aid I can access with an ITIN?

Readers frequently ask:

State-level aid has expanded significantly since 2020. Some states, like California, Texas, and New York, offer financial aid to undocumented students or Dreamers. Over a dozen states have passed their own versions of the Dream Act, creating pathways to state grants and tuition equity that operate entirely outside the federal FAFSA system.

California’s program is the most mature. The California Dream Act Application is a separate form from the FAFSA and opens access to Cal Grants, community college fee waivers, and some university institutional aid for students who meet California’s AB 540 criteria, regardless of immigration status or SSN. To access state aid in California, you complete the California Dream Act Application instead of the FAFSA.

Texas and New York have similar in-state tuition equity laws, and states like Illinois, Washington, Colorado, and New Mexico have added grant funding in recent years. The landscape changes annually, so because financial aid policies for immigrants vary widely by state, start by researching local programs through your state’s higher education agency or your school’s financial aid office.

If you’re already in college, your college’s financial aid office can guide you through state-specific options and private lending opportunities, this is especially true at community colleges and public universities in those states, where staff typically have experience with ITIN-holder applications.

What documents do I need to apply for a student loan with an ITIN?

The documentation stack for an ITIN-based student loan is similar to any private loan, with a few additions:

  • Your IRS ITIN assignment letter (Form CP565 is preferred; CP567 for renewals is also acceptable)
  • Passport or consular ID as primary photo identification
  • Proof of school enrollment, acceptance letter or current enrollment verification
  • Cost of attendance documentation from your school’s financial aid office
  • Any U.S. credit history, even a few months of on-time payments on an ITIN credit card or a credit-builder account strengthens your file meaningfully
  • Cosigner documents (if applicable): your cosigner will need their SSN, credit history, and proof of income

For visa holders, your visa type affects approval, H-1B, L-1, and other employment-based visas are easier to get approved for than tourist or short-term visas. For student visa holders (F-1), specialty lenders like MPOWER have purpose-built programs.

If you don’t yet have an ITIN, you can apply using IRS Form W-7. The IRS will assign you an ITIN regardless of immigration status, using documents like driver’s licenses, immigration papers, passports, and birth certificates to confirm your identity, and the ITIN you receive can be used to apply for student loans without an SSN.

Will getting a student loan with my ITIN help me build credit for the future?

This is one of the most underappreciated benefits of borrowing the right way. For lenders, an ITIN serves the same identification function as an SSN: it lets them pull a credit report, verify your identity, and report the loan to credit bureaus. That means every on-time payment on an ITIN-based student loan builds your U.S. credit history, the same file that will determine your rates when you later apply for an ITIN car loan, a personal loan, or eventually an ITIN mortgage.

The critical caveat: not every ITIN-accepting lender reports to all three bureaus. According to consumer finance researchers, some specialty immigrant lenders only report to one bureau or don’t report at all, which means you could make years of on-time payments and see no improvement in your scored credit file. Always confirm reporting practices before signing.

If you’re just starting out and have no U.S. credit history at all, pairing a student loan with a secured ITIN credit card that reports to all three bureaus is the fastest way to build a file that mainstream lenders will recognize. Even three to six months of on-time payments creates a “thin but workable” profile that improves your options considerably.

What are the red flags to watch for with ITIN student loan offers?

A question we hear often:

ITIN borrowers are, unfortunately, a frequent target for predatory lenders. Predatory lenders target people with limited credit access, and people without SSNs are often in this group. When evaluating any student loan offer, watch for these warning signs:

  • No state lending license, verify the lender is licensed in your state through the NMLS Consumer Access database
  • Fees collected before disbursement, legitimate lenders do not charge upfront fees before you receive loan funds
  • APR not clearly disclosed, any lender unwilling to quote you an Annual Percentage Rate in writing before you sign is a red flag
  • Loan terms longer than your program, lenders will generally only allow you to choose a repayment term that’s shorter than the period of time you’re authorized to live in the U.S., since this reduces collection risk
  • Pressure to sign quickly, legitimate lenders give you time to compare

Always borrow from licensed lenders and institutions, and verify licensing with your state’s financial regulator. If a deal sounds unusually easy or cheap compared to what other ITIN lenders are offering, treat that as a signal to slow down and read every line.

How does a cosigner change my chances?

Securing a cosigner is one of the most important steps you can take, you may be able to find a private lender that won’t require one, but it’s certainly easier to find private loans if you have one, and you’ll likely pay lower interest rates.

A cosigner for a student loan doesn’t need to be a parent, it can be any U.S. citizen or permanent resident with good credit who is willing to take on legal responsibility for the debt if you cannot pay. In practice, most private lenders require a U.S. citizen or permanent resident cosigner with good credit when the primary borrower lacks an SSN.

If finding a cosigner is a barrier, MPOWER Financing and a handful of CDFIs have specifically built their underwriting models around borrowers who don’t have one. These loans typically carry higher interest rates in exchange for that flexibility, so if a creditworthy cosigner is available to you, using one will almost always produce better terms. Once you’ve made 24-36 months of on-time payments and built a solid ITIN credit file, many lenders allow cosigner release, removing your sponsor from the obligation while you keep the loan.

Common questions

Can I apply for federal student loans with an ITIN?

No. Federal student aid through FAFSA requires a Social Security number. ITIN holders, including undocumented students and DACA recipients, are not eligible for federal Stafford, Pell Grant, or PLUS loan programs. You must pursue private lenders or state-specific aid instead.

Which private lenders accept an ITIN for student loans?

Lenders that openly work with ITIN borrowers include Ascent Funding, MPOWER Financing (for F-1 and DACA students), and several credit unions in high-immigrant-population states. Most require a creditworthy U.S. cosigner, though MPOWER and a few others offer cosigner-free options.

Do I need a cosigner to get a student loan with an ITIN?

Most private lenders that accept ITINs still require a cosigner who is a U.S. citizen or permanent resident with good credit. However, a small number of specialty lenders, including MPOWER Financing, will underwrite loans based on academic trajectory and earning potential rather than requiring a cosigner.

What states offer student aid to ITIN holders or undocumented students?

As of 2026, California, Texas, New York, Illinois, Washington, and over a dozen other states offer state-funded financial aid or tuition equity to undocumented students regardless of ITIN or SSN status. California's Dream Act Application is the most robust, replacing FAFSA for eligible undocumented and DACA students in the state.

Will taking out a student loan with my ITIN help build my credit?

It can, if the lender reports to the major credit bureaus. Ask any private lender upfront whether they report to Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax under your ITIN. On-time student loan payments can meaningfully strengthen your U.S. credit file, making it easier to qualify for other ITIN loans like auto or personal loans later.

Can a DACA recipient get a student loan?

Yes. DACA recipients are not eligible for federal student loans but can apply for private student loans with an ITIN, cosigner-free programs like MPOWER, and state aid in roughly 20 states that have passed their own Dream Acts. Income-based scholarships and institutional aid from colleges are also worth exploring.
