FHA-Style 3.5% Down Payment Loans for ITIN Borrowers (2026)

By Priya Sharma · Loans & Banking Writer · Published July 6, 2026

Quick Answer

Standard FHA loans generally require a valid Social Security number, so ITIN holders don't qualify for a direct HUD-insured FHA loan. A partner lending program now offers ITIN borrowers a similar 3.5% down payment structure through a non-QM portfolio product modeled on FHA guidelines. Availability, rate, and terms depend on the lender and your file, not on FHA itself.

If you’ve read our guide on qualifying for an ITIN mortgage, you already know the standard down payment for an ITIN home loan runs 10-20%. A partner lending program now offers a narrower alternative: a 3.5% down payment option built specifically for ITIN borrowers, modeled on the same minimum FHA loans use. It’s a genuinely different number, but it’s important to understand exactly what kind of loan this is, and isn’t, before you count on it.

Wait, does the FHA actually lend to ITIN holders now?

No, and this is worth being precise about. A standard FHA loan, insured directly by HUD, requires the borrower to have a valid Social Security number and documented lawful residency or work-eligibility status. An Individual Taxpayer Identification Number does not, by itself, satisfy that requirement. HUD Handbook 4000.1 sets the borrower-eligibility rules for FHA-insured loans, and an ITIN-only file does not meet them.

What exists instead is a partner lending program built to offer ITIN borrowers the same 3.5% minimum down payment FHA is known for, without being an actual HUD-insured FHA loan. It’s a portfolio product, the lender holds the loan on its own books rather than selling it into an FHA-backed pool, structured to mirror FHA’s low-down-payment threshold.

If that distinction sounds like a technicality, it isn’t, it determines who backs the loan, what happens if the loan is sold or refinanced, and what recourse exists if something goes wrong. Ask the lender directly whether the loan is FHA-insured or a portfolio product before you apply; a program styled after FHA is not the same as an FHA loan.

Why does a 3.5% down payment for ITIN borrowers matter?

Every other ITIN mortgage program we track, including standard non-QM options detailed in our guide on how to qualify for an ITIN mortgage, sits at 10-20% down for a primary residence. A small number of specialty lenders have advertised programs as low as 10%, but 20% remains the common baseline.

A 3.5% option changes the math meaningfully. On a $300,000 home, the difference between 20% down ($60,000) and 3.5% down ($10,500) is roughly $49,500, often the single biggest barrier standing between an ITIN holder and a signed contract. For borrowers with strong income and credit but limited cash reserves, a lower down-payment threshold can be the difference between buying this year and waiting several more years to save.

Who is likely to qualify for the 3.5% down program?

How do I find out if I qualify?

The fastest way to check is through our lead form, where you tell us you’re interested in the FHA-style 3.5%-down program and we connect you with the partner lender directly. This does not affect your credit, checking eligibility is not a formal application, and you are never obligated to move forward.

Before you start, it helps to have a rough sense of your numbers ready: your approximate annual income, whether you’re W-2 or self-employed, whether you have a U.S. credit score, and how much you have saved for a down payment and closing costs. None of this needs to be exact; the point is to have a starting conversation, not to submit a full application on the first call.

What if I don’t qualify for the 3.5% program?

If the partner program’s overlays don’t fit your file, that doesn’t mean ITIN home financing is off the table, it means you’re back to the broader ITIN mortgage market covered in our complete guide to qualifying for an ITIN mortgage, where 10-20% down remains the standard. It’s also worth exploring down payment assistance options for ITIN borrowers, which can supplement whatever down payment threshold you’re working with, whether that’s 3.5% or 20%.

Rates on any ITIN mortgage, FHA-style or standard, tend to run 0.5-2 percentage points above conventional conforming loans, reflecting the non-QM/portfolio structure. That premium applies here too; a lower down payment does not mean a lower rate.

Common questions

Does the FHA actually accept an ITIN instead of a Social Security number?

No. Per HUD Handbook 4000.1, FHA-insured loans require the borrower to have a valid Social Security number and lawful residency or work eligibility status. An ITIN alone does not meet FHA's standard borrower-eligibility requirements. The 3.5%-down program described here is a separate, non-QM product a partner lender designed to mirror FHA's down payment structure for ITIN borrowers, not a HUD-insured FHA loan.

If it's not a real FHA loan, why is it called an 'FHA-style' program?

Because the defining feature borrowers care about, a 3.5% minimum down payment, comes from FHA's guidelines and this program was built to match it. The underlying loan is a portfolio (non-QM) product held by the lender, the same category as other ITIN mortgages, just structured with FHA's low down payment threshold instead of the typical 10-20% ITIN baseline.

Who qualifies for the 3.5% down payment program?

Qualification is set by the partner lender, not by us, and works similarly to other ITIN mortgage programs: a valid ITIN, income documentation (W-2 or self-employed bank statements/1099s), and either a qualifying U.S. credit score or documented alternative credit. Because it's a specialty program, expect the lender to apply its own overlays on top of the base 3.5% down structure. Confirm current eligibility directly with the partner before you count on this option.

How is this different from the other ITIN mortgage programs on this site?

Most ITIN mortgage programs we cover require 10-20% down (see our guide on how to qualify for an ITIN mortgage). This program is notable specifically because it lowers that threshold to 3.5%, matching FHA's minimum. Everything else, rate premiums versus conventional loans, non-QM classification, portfolio-lender underwriting, works the same way as any other ITIN mortgage.

Is this program available in every state?

Availability varies by lender licensing and program rules, which change. Don't assume it's available in your state without confirming directly through the application process; state-by-state availability is exactly the kind of detail that shifts without public announcement.

Will applying affect my immigration status?

No. Mortgage lenders do not report loan applications to immigration enforcement agencies, and the Equal Credit Opportunity Act prohibits lenders from discriminating based on national origin or immigration status.
