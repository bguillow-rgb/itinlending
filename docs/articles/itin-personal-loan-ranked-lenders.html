Getting approved for a personal loan without a Social Security number is possible, but the outcome depends almost entirely on which lender you approach. No federal law prohibits lenders from issuing personal loans to borrowers without Social Security numbers. The catch is that not all lenders accept ITINs, and most traditional banks and large credit unions are SSN-only.

What is an ITIN personal loan, exactly?

ITIN personal loans are installment loans that give you a lump sum repaid over a fixed period through regular monthly payments. From the borrower’s side, they work the same as traditional personal loans. The only difference is the type of identification the lender accepts during the application.

For lenders, an ITIN does the same job as an SSN: it lets them pull a credit report, verify your identity, and report the loan to credit bureaus. That last part matters more than most borrowers realize. Every on-time payment on a reported ITIN loan builds your U.S. credit file, which opens the door to lower rates later. Before applying, it is worth pulling your free credit report at AnnualCreditReport.com to see what credit history, if any, already exists under your ITIN.

Loan amounts generally run from about $500 to $35,000. Borrowers with limited credit history often start in the $1,000-$5,000 range. Those with established credit, consistent income, and a solid banking history may qualify for higher limits, depending on the lender.

Who actually approves ITIN personal loans in 2026?

A question we hear often: There are four distinct categories of lenders willing to work with ITIN borrowers, and they differ significantly in rate, eligibility, and loan size.

Many local and regional credit unions accept ITINs, especially in states with large immigrant populations. Self-Help Federal Credit Union, Latino Community Credit Union, and Mission Asset Fund are good examples. Membership is usually based on geography or community ties.

Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs) are nonprofit lenders certified by the U.S. Treasury that focus on underbanked communities. They typically accept ITINs and carry lower interest rates than online lenders. Oportun itself holds CDFI certification, which is why its rates are capped lower than typical subprime lenders.

Online fintech lenders that explicitly accept ITINs include MoneyLion (for some products through its credit-builder loan), EzLoan (personal loans up to $5,000 for thin-file and ITIN borrowers), Camino Financial (for business-focused borrowers), and Stilt (personal loans aimed at visa holders and immigrants).

A small number of major banks accept ITINs under specific conditions. Wells Fargo accepts ITIN applications and offers low APRs with large loan amounts and long repayment periods, but only for existing customers with an account open at least 12 months. Most U.S. national banks require an SSN. A few branches will accept ITINs for select products if you have an existing relationship, but that is not a reliable path to count on.

What APR should I realistically expect?

This one comes up a lot: The short answer is that it depends on your credit profile and which lender type you approach.

Interest rates on ITIN personal loans run higher than comparable SSN-based loans. Typical APRs fall in the 12%-36% range, with some specialty lenders charging more. Credit strength matters a lot here. Borrowers with scores above 680 and stable income generally get better rates than those with thin credit files or limited repayment history.

A $5,000 loan at 24% APR over 36 months carries monthly payments around $196 and total interest of about $2,068. The same loan at a credit union or CDFI charging 14% APR drops that to roughly $171 per month and $1,156 in total interest. That $900 difference is exactly why it pays to try a credit union first before going to a fintech lender.

Which specific lenders accept ITINs and what are their actual terms?

Readers frequently ask: Rather than a generic list, here is what each verified lender actually offers ITIN borrowers as of mid-2026.

Security Service Federal Credit Union is one of the most competitive options for ITIN holders with established credit. You can borrow $250 to $50,000 with a repayment period of 3 to 66 months, and the APR range runs 9.99%-17.95%. Anyone who lives in the United States or a U.S. territory can become eligible to join through a free membership with the American Consumer Council, a nonprofit consumer education organization. That wide eligibility makes this accessible to ITIN holders in most states.

Oportun is the most accessible lender for borrowers with no U.S. credit history. Oportun has no minimum credit score requirement and accepts applicants with limited or no credit history. You need an SSN or ITIN, plus proof of residency and employment or income. Loan amounts run $300-$10,000 unsecured, and up to $18,500 secured in states including AZ, CA, FL, IL, NV, NJ, TX, and UT. Oportun caps personal loan APRs at 35.99% and charges an origination fee of 10%, which is worth factoring into your total cost. On the upside, most loans are funded the same day, and Oportun operates in 41 states.

OneMain Financial accepts ITIN borrowers and has close to 1,400 branches across 44 states. APRs run 11.99%-35.99%, and loan amounts range from $1,500 to $30,000 with payoff periods of 24, 36, 48, or 60 months. OneMain works with borrowers across the credit spectrum, from the high 500s to excellent, though you will need at least one active credit account to qualify. One thing to know: OneMain’s standard application asks for a Social Security number. ITIN holders should call their local branch in advance to confirm ITIN acceptance before submitting a formal application, since branch-level policies can vary.

PNC Bank offers personal loans up to $35,000 with an APR as low as 8.14% and explicitly accepts ITINs. This is one of the rare major-bank options, but like Wells Fargo, an existing PNC banking relationship significantly improves your odds.

Self-Help Federal Credit Union and Latino Community Credit Union are among the CDFIs most consistently cited for ITIN-holder service. Membership is usually based on geography or community ties. Latino Community Credit Union, based in North Carolina but serving members in several southeastern states, is particularly well known for Spanish-language service and immigrant-inclusive underwriting.

Embold Credit Union in the Pacific Northwest explicitly markets its ITIN loan program. The program is designed for community members who have ITINs and not Social Security numbers. To qualify, you need an ITIN letter and must live, work, worship, or attend school in Clackamas, Multnomah, Washington, Marion, Yamhill, or Polk County, or be a family member of a current member. Embold offers low fixed rates, easy payments, and no prepayment penalties.

Resource One Credit Union (Texas) is worth knowing about for Dallas-area ITIN holders. Resource One accepts the ITIN as a valid form of identification and ITIN borrowers receive the same terms and conditions as those with a Social Security number, which is unusual and genuinely useful. Membership is open to anyone who lives or works in Mesquite, Garland, Irving, Cedar Hill, and Dallas County in Texas.

What documents do I need to apply?

A question we hear often: Missing or inconsistent documents are the most common reason ITIN loan applications stall. Pull everything together before you contact any lender.

Your ITIN letter proves the IRS assigned your tax identification number. The original CP565 letter works best, though the CP567 renewal confirmation is also accepted. If you have lost your letter, request a replacement from the IRS. That takes several weeks, so plan ahead.

Government-issued identification verifies who you are. Foreign passports are widely accepted, as are consular identification cards and some state-issued IDs that do not require SSNs.

Beyond identification, most lenders will want:

  • Recent pay stubs (last 30-60 days) or 2-3 months of bank statements
  • Proof of U.S. address, such as a utility bill or signed lease agreement
  • Tax returns from the past one to two years (especially if self-employed)
  • Visa documents (I-94, visa stamp, or EAD card) if you hold a U.S. visa

H-1B, L-1, and other employment-based visas are easier to get approved for than tourist or short-term visas. A visa with remaining validity longer than the loan term strengthens your application considerably.

What are my approval odds if I have little or no U.S. credit history?

This is where realistic expectations matter. Without a U.S. credit file, your options narrow, but they do not disappear.

Without an SSN and with a thin or nonexistent U.S. credit file, expect APRs of 10%-36% (credit unions at the low end, fintech subprime lenders at the high end) and loan amounts of $1,000-$25,000, with most lenders capping at $10,000-$15,000. If you have strong income, established employment, and a U.S. credit file built over a year or more, you can often qualify in the 10%-20% APR range.

Several factors can offset a thin credit file. Lenders assess income stability, credit history, and repayment capacity rather than visa category, so documenting consistent income is as important as your credit score. If you have time, build U.S. credit before you apply. Even three to six months of on-time payments on a credit-builder card gives you a thin but workable file that noticeably improves your loan options.

Alternative credit references can also help with lenders that accept them. Some ITIN lenders will consider a history of paying utility bills and rent in place of, or alongside, a traditional credit score.

According to the Urban Institute, most lenders that offer ITIN loans are credit unions and community development financial institutions. Your first call should almost always be to a local credit union or CDFI, not a national lender’s website.

Should I choose a secured or unsecured ITIN personal loan?

For borrowers with thin credit files, a secured ITIN personal loan can be a smart move. A secured loan uses collateral (usually a vehicle or a savings account) to reduce the lender’s risk.

OneMain offers secured personal loans and accepts vehicles less than 10 years old, including cars, trucks, motorcycles, RVs, and boats. A secured loan can help borrowers qualify for better terms, but OneMain can seize your collateral if you do not repay.

At Oportun, securing a personal loan with a vehicle can increase the likelihood of approval or getting a larger loan. The maximum loan amount is $18,500 for a secured loan with a qualifying, paid-off vehicle as collateral.

The tradeoff is real: if you default, you lose the collateral. Only pledge collateral you can afford to lose. That said, if you have solid income but no credit history, a secured loan is often the fastest path to getting approved and building a credit record at the same time.

Readers frequently ask: Because ITIN borrowers often have limited alternatives, predatory lenders specifically target this population.

Step-by-step: how to apply for an ITIN personal loan

The right sequence saves you from unnecessary hard credit inquiries and improves your approval odds.

Step 1: Pull your credit report first. Get a free credit report at AnnualCreditReport.com to see what U.S. credit history, if any, exists under your ITIN. Lenders assess your debt-to-income ratio to determine whether you can reasonably take on another monthly obligation. High existing debt relative to income can limit approval amounts or lead to a denial.

Step 2: Gather your documents. Collect your IRS ITIN letter, passport, proof of income, and proof of address before you contact any lender. Missing or inconsistent documents are a common reason applications stall or get declined.

Step 3: Apply at a credit union or CDFI first. Local credit unions and CDFIs tend to have the most flexible ITIN policies and the lowest rates. If you cannot qualify locally, fintech lenders like Stilt and EzLoan focus specifically on ITIN and immigrant borrowers.

Step 4: Use soft-pull pre-qualification before committing. Use a marketplace such as Upstart or LendingTree for soft-pull comparisons before any hard pull. Oportun also lets you check your approval odds and potential rates online without affecting your credit score.

Step 5: Compare total cost, not just monthly payment. A lower monthly payment from a longer term often means far more interest paid overall. Use the lender’s own calculator to compute total interest over the life of the loan before you sign.

How does my visa type affect ITIN personal loan approval?

Immigration status and loan approval are separate questions legally, but visa type does matter in practice. ITINs are issued to a range of individuals, including certain visa holders, non-resident aliens with U.S. income, and dependents required to file U.S. tax returns who do not qualify for Social Security numbers. Lenders assess income stability, credit history, and repayment capacity rather than visa category.

That said, lenders use visa validity as a proxy for risk. H-1B, L-1, and other employment-based visas are easier to get approved for than tourist or short-term visas. A lender generally wants the visa to remain valid at least as long as the loan term, so a 24-month loan is much easier to approve than a 60-month loan if your visa expires in 18 months.

For undocumented borrowers without any visa, the ITIN itself is the primary identification, and the focus shifts entirely to income verification, banking history, and available credit references. In that situation, CDFIs and community credit unions are almost always the right starting point.

Can an ITIN personal loan actually help me build credit?

Yes, when the lender reports to the bureaus. Credit bureaus track your file under your ITIN the same way they track an SSN holder’s file. Ask the lender directly whether they report before you sign.

A successfully repaid personal loan adds a positive installment trade line to your credit report, strengthening your credit mix and payment history, the two most heavily weighted factors in most scoring models. Borrowers who take out a small ITIN personal loan, repay it on time, and apply again 12 months later consistently report better terms the second time.

For those not yet ready for a personal loan, a credit builder loan with an ITIN is often the better first step. If you already have some U.S. credit history and want to understand your score, our guide to checking your credit score with an ITIN walks through the process.

Do ITIN personal loans affect my immigration status? No. Applying for or receiving a personal loan does not affect immigration proceedings. ITINs are issued by the IRS strictly for tax purposes, and lender applications are entirely separate from immigration records.

Common questions

Can I get a personal loan with only an ITIN and no SSN?

Yes. No federal law prohibits lenders from issuing personal loans to borrowers without a Social Security number. An ITIN satisfies lenders' identity verification and tax-reporting requirements. Credit unions, CDFIs, and several fintech lenders accept ITINs in place of an SSN.

What credit score do I need for an ITIN personal loan?

Requirements vary by lender. Credit unions and CDFIs typically want a score of 620 or above, or will consider alternative credit (rent, utility payments). Oportun has no minimum credit score. OneMain Financial considers all credit scores. Borrowers with scores above 680 generally get the best rates.

What APR can I expect on a personal loan with an ITIN?

Typical APRs range from 8%-18% at credit unions and CDFIs, and 12%-36% at fintech and storefront lenders. Borrowers with limited U.S. credit history tend to land in the 20%-36% range. Building even three to six months of credit history before applying can noticeably lower your rate.

How much can I borrow with an ITIN personal loan?

Loan amounts typically run from $300 to $35,000 depending on the lender. Borrowers new to U.S. credit usually start with $1,000-$5,000. Those with established income and credit history may qualify for up to $35,000 through lenders like PNC or Security Service Federal Credit Union.

What documents do I need to apply for a personal loan with an ITIN?

You will typically need your IRS ITIN assignment letter (Form CP565 or CP567), a government-issued photo ID such as a foreign passport or consular ID, recent pay stubs or 2-3 months of bank statements, proof of U.S. address (utility bill or lease), and visa documents if you are a visa holder.

Does getting an ITIN personal loan help build my credit?

Yes, as long as the lender reports to the major credit bureaus. Credit bureaus track your file under your ITIN the same way they track an SSN holder's file. Always confirm whether the lender reports to Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax before signing.

Can I get an ITIN personal loan with bad credit?

Yes. Lenders like Oportun, OneMain Financial, and many CDFIs work with borrowers who have bad or thin credit. A secured loan (using a vehicle or savings as collateral) can improve your approval odds and potentially lower your rate. See our full guide to ITIN loans with bad credit for details.

Are there ITIN personal loan lenders that serve Spanish speakers?

Yes. Oportun, Latino Community Credit Union, Self-Help Federal Credit Union, Apoyo Financiero, and many local CDFIs offer Spanish-language service. Upward Credit Union and several community credit unions also provide loan assistance in Spanish.

How long does it take to get funded on an ITIN personal loan?

Funding timelines vary. Oportun typically funds the same day. OneMain Financial says most borrowers receive same- or next-day funding after approval. Credit unions can take 2-5 business days due to manual underwriting. Fintech lenders generally fund within 1-3 business days.

What is the difference between a secured and unsecured ITIN personal loan?

An unsecured loan requires no collateral and is based entirely on your creditworthiness. A secured loan requires collateral, usually a vehicle or savings account, which reduces the lender's risk and typically results in a lower interest rate and higher loan amount. Secured options are useful for borrowers with thin credit files.
