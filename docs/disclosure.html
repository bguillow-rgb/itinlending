How we earn revenue

We have a few revenue streams, and every page on this site is funded by one or more of them:

  • Lender and partner referrals. When you submit our qualification form and we connect you with a third-party lender or financing partner, we may receive a referral fee or be paid when you complete an application or fund a loan. The form passes your request to a matching partner; what you submit is used to connect you with lenders that work with ITIN holders.
  • Affiliate links through Commission Junction (CJ). Many of our outbound "apply" and "get matched" links to specific lenders, credit cards, and financing products are affiliate links served through the Commission Junction (CJ Affiliate) network. When you click one of these links and then qualify, sign up, or open an account, the advertiser pays us a commission. The link carries a tracking code that tells CJ the referral came from us; it does not transmit your personal information to us.
  • Affiliate display advertising through Awin. Some of the banner units on the site, including the Credit Karma unit you may see on certain pages, are served through the Awin affiliate network. We are paid when you click the unit and complete an action on the advertiser's site (for example, signing up for a free account). These units are always labeled "Advertisement."
  • Display advertising through Google AdSense. On our educational articles we run contextual display ads served by Google AdSense and its partners. We are paid based on impressions and clicks. We do not control which specific advertiser appears in an AdSense slot, Google's system selects those, and their presence is not an endorsement by us.

How compensation affects (and does not affect) what you read

This is the part that matters most. Being paid by an advertiser can influence which products and lenders we choose to write about and where on a page a product link appears. It does not change the facts we report, the trade-offs we disclose, or our assessment of whether a product is right for a given situation. We do not accept payment to change a recommendation, to remove a drawback from a comparison, or to call a weak product a strong one. Our editorial conclusions are reached before, and independently of, any commercial arrangement, and they would read exactly the same if we earned nothing from them.

How we select and rank products

Products, lenders, and financing options are chosen for coverage based on how relevant and accessible they are to ITIN holders, specifically whether a lender accepts an ITIN in place of a Social Security Number, its documentation and down-payment requirements, its real costs and rate ranges, and how suitable it is for someone borrowing without an SSN or a long U.S. credit history. A product is never included simply because it pays the highest commission, and a relevant option is never excluded simply because it pays us nothing or has no affiliate program at all. Where we compare options, the attributes in our tables (down payments, rate ranges, documentation, fees) reflect publicly documented lender terms, which can change; we tell you to verify the current terms directly with the lender before you apply.
