Quick Answer

To get an ITIN, file IRS Form W-7 with proof of identity and foreign status (a passport is ideal), usually along with a federal tax return. Submit by mail, through an IRS-authorized Certifying Acceptance Agent, or at a Taxpayer Assistance Center. Processing takes about 7–11 weeks. The IRS charges no fee for the form itself.

What is an ITIN?

An Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) is a nine-digit tax processing number the IRS issues to people who must file U.S. taxes but aren't eligible for a Social Security Number. It always begins with a 9. An ITIN doesn't grant work authorization or immigration status — but it does open the door to banking, credit, and loans.

Step-by-step: how to apply

  1. Complete IRS Form W-7 — the "Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number."
  2. Gather your documents — a valid passport is the simplest, since it proves both identity and foreign status. Without one, you'll need two alternative documents.
  3. Attach your tax return — most applicants must include a completed federal return, unless they meet an exception.
  4. Submit your application — by mail to the IRS, through a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA), or in person at an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center.
  5. Wait for your ITIN — the IRS mails it in about 7–11 weeks.

After you get your ITIN

Once your ITIN arrives, you can file taxes, open a U.S. bank account, apply for credit cards, and pursue the loans covered across this site. A smart first move is opening a bank account and a secured credit card to start building U.S. credit history right away.

Common questions

How do I apply for an ITIN?

File IRS Form W-7 with proof of identity and foreign status, usually alongside a federal tax return. You can submit by mail, through an IRS-authorized Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA), or at an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center.

How long does it take to get an ITIN?

The IRS typically processes ITIN applications in about 7 to 11 weeks, and longer during tax season (January–April). Applying outside peak season is faster.

What documents do I need for an ITIN?

A completed Form W-7, a valid passport (or two alternative IDs), and usually a federal tax return. A passport is the only document that proves both identity and foreign status on its own.

Does it cost money to get an ITIN?

The IRS does not charge a fee for Form W-7 itself. Certifying Acceptance Agents and tax preparers may charge for their help, but you can also apply directly for free.

Do I need to renew my ITIN?

ITINs not used on a federal tax return for three consecutive years expire, as do certain older ITINs. If yours has expired, file Form W-7 to renew before using it on a return.
