Can you get a loan with an ITIN in Maryland?

Yes. ITIN holders across Maryland can qualify for mortgages, auto loans, personal loans, business loans, and credit cards without a Social Security number. Lenders that serve Maryland use your Individual Taxpayer Identification Number to verify identity and pull credit, then qualify you on income, down payment, and payment history. Maryland is one of the largest ITIN markets in the country — undocumented residents alone paid an estimated $779 million in Maryland state and local taxes in 2022, at an effective rate of 8.7% (Institute on Taxation and Economic Policy). A consistent tax-filing record under your ITIN is exactly the documentation Maryland lenders want to see.

What ITIN loans are available in Maryland?

  • ITIN mortgages — buy a home in Maryland with no SSN, typically 10–20% down.
  • ITIN auto loans — finance a car through Maryland credit unions and dealers.
  • ITIN personal loans — cover emergencies and large expenses.
  • ITIN business loans — fund and grow a Maryland business.
  • ITIN credit cards — start building U.S. credit history.

Can ITIN holders get a driver's license in Maryland?

Yes. Maryland issues a standard driver's license regardless of immigration status (a policy enacted in 2013). That matters for an ITIN auto loan: a state license makes it far easier to register, insure, and finance a vehicle, and many Maryland dealerships and credit unions actively work with ITIN borrowers who hold one. If you want a car, getting your license and your financing pre-approved together is the smoothest path.

Building credit in Maryland with an ITIN

Filing taxes with an ITIN does more than meet IRS rules — it builds the paper trail Maryland lenders rely on. ITIN filers are part of the $779 million in state and local taxes undocumented residents contributed in Maryland in 2022. Pair consistent tax returns with on-time payments on an ITIN credit card or auto loan — reported to all three bureaus — and you build the Maryland credit history that unlocks lower mortgage and loan rates. Start with one reporting account, keep balances low, and never miss a due date.

Common questions

Can I get a mortgage with an ITIN in Maryland?

Yes. ITIN mortgages are available to Maryland residents through portfolio lenders and credit unions that keep loans in-house. Expect roughly 10–20% down and 12–24 months of income documentation in place of an SSN.

Do I need a Social Security number to get a loan in Maryland?

No. Lenders serving Maryland accept your Individual Taxpayer Identification Number in place of an SSN. Enter your ITIN wherever the application asks for a Social Security number.

Can I finance a car with an ITIN in Maryland?

Yes. Because Maryland issues driver's licenses regardless of immigration status, financing, registering, and insuring a car is straightforward. Many Maryland dealers and credit unions work with ITIN borrowers.

Do ITIN loans build credit in Maryland?

Yes, when the lender reports to Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion. On-time payments on an ITIN credit card, auto loan, or personal loan build the Maryland credit history that unlocks better rates later.
