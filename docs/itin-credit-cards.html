Quick Answer

Yes, you can get a credit card with an ITIN. Many issuers and credit unions accept an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number in place of an SSN. Secured cards — which use a refundable deposit as your limit — are the easiest entry point. Paying on time builds U.S. credit history under your ITIN, often upgrading you to an unsecured card.

Can you get a credit card with an ITIN?

Yes. You don't need a Social Security Number to get a U.S. credit card. Many issuers and most credit unions accept an ITIN, and the application often has a field where you can enter it in place of an SSN. If you're new to U.S. credit, a secured card is almost always the smartest first move.

Start secured, pay on time for several months, and most issuers will either raise your limit or graduate you to an unsecured card — returning your deposit.

How to build credit fast with an ITIN card

  • Pay in full and on time — payment history is the biggest factor in your score.
  • Keep utilization under 30% — and ideally under 10% — of your limit.
  • Don't close your first card — length of history helps your score.
  • Add accounts gradually — a card plus a small loan builds a fuller file.

Open a bank account first

Most ITIN holders find approval easier after opening a U.S. checking or savings account with their ITIN. It establishes a banking relationship and gives you a simple way to autopay your card — which protects the on-time payment streak that powers your credit growth.

Common questions

Can I get a credit card with an ITIN?

Yes. Several major issuers and many credit unions let you apply for a credit card using an ITIN instead of an SSN. Secured cards are the easiest starting point if you have little or no U.S. credit history.

Which banks accept an ITIN for credit cards?

Policies change, but several large national banks and many community banks and credit unions accept ITINs. Some applications have an SSN field you can use your ITIN in; others ask for the ITIN directly. We help match you with current ITIN-friendly options.

What is a secured credit card?

A secured card requires a refundable deposit that becomes your credit limit. It is the most reliable way to start building credit with an ITIN, and many convert to unsecured cards after several months of on-time payments.

Will an ITIN credit card build my credit?

Yes, as long as the issuer reports to the major bureaus — most do. Keeping your balance low and paying on time builds U.S. credit history under your ITIN quickly.

Do I need a bank account first?

Usually yes. Opening a U.S. checking or savings account with your ITIN first makes credit card approval smoother and gives you a way to make payments.
