ITIN lending: common questions

Can I get a loan with an ITIN instead of an SSN?

Yes. Many banks, credit unions, and non-QM lenders accept an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) in place of a Social Security Number for mortgages, auto loans, personal loans, business loans, and credit cards. Requirements vary by lender and product.

Do I need a green card or visa to qualify?

No. ITIN lending exists specifically for people without an SSN, including those without permanent residency. Lenders focus on income, down payment, and credit history rather than immigration status. Exact eligibility depends on the lender.

How much down payment do I need for an ITIN mortgage?

Most ITIN mortgages require 10% to 20% down. A larger down payment can offset a thin credit file and unlock better rates. Some lenders go lower for strong borrowers; the median in 2026 sits around 15%.

Will ITIN loans hurt my credit score?

Getting matched and comparing options on this site does not affect your credit. A lender only runs a hard credit check once you formally apply. Paying an ITIN loan on time actually helps you build U.S. credit history.

Is ITINLending.net a lender?

No. We are an independent information and referral resource. We publish guides and connect ITIN holders with third-party lenders. We do not make credit decisions, set rates, or originate loans.
